2.2 Measures to maximise impact - Dissemination, exploitation and communication
2.2.1 Dissemination of results
The dissemination of project results is fundamental to the success of the project. It is important that the project’s results reach all relevant stakeholders in order for the project to achieve its objectives. The project’s results will be disseminated to academia, industry, the general public and other stakeholders of the project, in particular, the food industry. The project’s dissemination activities will be carried out by all partners. Key measures to reach the relevant target groups are:
• Publications in peer-reviewed scientific journals and presentations at scientific conferences. For example, the use of NIR spectroscopy for food quality control is an emerging field with a high technical impact. The results obtained in the project will be of high interest to the scientific community in food science and technology, analytical chemistry, spectroscopy, food safety and quality, food process control, and food packaging.
• Demonstrations of the developed technology and the developed prototypes at conferences and exhibitions.
• Exploitation of results through the partners’ own channels and networks. The project partners will use their own websites as well as social media to disseminate the results.
• Publications in trade journals and magazines, e.g. Food Manufacture, Food Navigator, The Grocer, Food Safety Magazine, Food Production Daily, Food Science and Technology, Food Engineering, Food Market Europe.
• Presentations at trade and industry conferences and exhibitions such as Food Matters Live, Food and Drink Expo, Food and Drink Technology Europe, Food Ingredients Europe, Food Ingredients North America, and Food Quality Europe.
• Organisation of workshops and trade shows.
• Targeted presentations at workshops, conferences and trade shows.
• Dissemination of results in press releases and other media.
• Use of the project’s website and social media channels for dissemination.
• Publications in the project’s own Newsletter.
