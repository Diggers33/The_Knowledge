=== Domain B: photonic sensors for pharmaceutical inline process analytical technology (PAT) ===
=== Section 2_1: Expected outcomes and impacts ===

1.1. Expected impacts:

1.1.1. Impact: Improved competitiveness of EU industry and SMEs through the development of new products and services
Our project will develop new photonic sensors for pharmaceutical inline PAT that will enable the pharmaceutical industry to produce high-quality medicines at a lower cost and with a shorter time-to-market. This will increase the competitiveness of the European pharmaceutical industry and strengthen its position in the global market. In addition, our project will create new jobs in the photonics and pharmaceutical industries and will contribute to the growth of these sectors.

1.1.2. Impact: Creation and expansion of markets for innovative products
Our project will develop new photonic sensors for pharmaceutical inline PAT that will be used in a variety of applications, such as drug development, drug manufacturing, and quality control. This will create new markets for our sensors and will expand the market for pharmaceutical inline PAT. In addition, our project will create new opportunities for the development of new products and services based on photonic sensors.

1.1.3. Impact: Unlocking the potential of key digital, enabling and emerging technologies, and their convergence, in particular microelectronics, photonics, nanotechnologies, advanced materials, advanced manufacturing technologies, biotechnology, Artificial Intelligence (AI), High-Performance Computing (HPC), Big Data and robotics
Our project will contribute to the unlocking of the potential of key digital, enabling and emerging technologies, in particular photonics, AI, HPC, and Big Data. Our sensors will use advanced photonic technologies, such as near-infrared (NIR) spectroscopy and machine learning algorithms, to monitor and control pharmaceutical processes in real-time. This will enable the pharmaceutical industry to produce high-quality medicines at a lower cost and with a shorter time-to-market.

1.1.4. Impact: Increased autonomy and resilience through the development of more efficient and sustainable technologies
Our project will develop new photonic sensors for pharmaceutical inline PAT that will be more efficient and sustainable than existing technologies. Our sensors will enable the pharmaceutical industry to produce medicines with less waste and emissions, and with a lower environmental impact. This will increase the autonomy and resilience of the European pharmaceutical industry and will contribute to the achievement of the European Green Deal objectives.

1.1.5. Impact: Increased innovation capacity and the uptake of digital technologies, in particular by SMEs
Our project will increase the innovation capacity of the European pharmaceutical industry by developing new photonic sensors for pharmaceutical inline PAT. Our sensors will enable the pharmaceutical industry to produce high-quality medicines at a lower cost and with a shorter time-to-market. This will increase the competitiveness of the European pharmaceutical industry and will create new opportunities for the development of new products and services. In addition, our project will increase the uptake of digital technologies, such as AI and HPC, by the pharmaceutical industry.

1.1.6. Impact: Contribution to the UN sustainable development goals
Our project will contribute to the UN sustainable development goals by increasing the quality of medicines, reducing the cost of healthcare, and reducing the environmental impact of the pharmaceutical industry. Our sensors will enable the pharmaceutical industry to produce high-quality medicines at a lower cost and with a shorter time-to-market. This will increase the accessibility of medicines and will reduce the burden of disease on society. In addition, our sensors will enable the pharmaceutical industry to produce medicines with less waste and emissions, and with a lower environmental impact.

1.2. Summary of the expected impacts

Our project will develop new photonic sensors for pharmaceutical inline PAT that will have a significant impact on the pharmaceutical industry and society. Our sensors will enable the pharmaceutical industry to produce high-quality medicines at a lower cost and with a shorter time-to-market. This will increase the competitiveness of the European pharmaceutical industry and will create new opportunities for the development of new products and services. In addition, our project will increase the innovation capacity of the European pharmaceutical industry and will contribute to the achievement of the European Green Deal objectives. Our project will also contribute to the UN sustainable development goals by increasing the quality of medicines, reducing the cost of healthcare, and reducing the environmental impact of the pharmaceutical industry.
