Objectives 
To develop efficient and comprehensive organizational, and technical management of the project. To Perform periodic 
reports on technical issues of the project to EC, define the technical dependencies among all the working groups and 
boundary conditions on use cases, and determine KPI’s to establish innovation potential of bio-based materials. 
 
Description of work 
Task 1.1. Scientific & Technical Coordination [M01-M36] [Lead: FHF; Participants: All] FHF will act as the 
technical coordinator of GREEN-LOOP. In this task, monitor and control the technical  development of the project 
will be performed. This comprises the co-ordination, supported by IDE, of technical progress, review and approval 
of technical reports and deliverables. The task covers all the activities to be carried out in the three value chains (WP3 
to WP5): i) Monitoring of the state of the art and of the state of practice. ii)Following of the progress of the scientific 
and technological developments of the project. iii)Identification of technical problems and remedial actions. 
Task 1.2. Working framework, requirements definition  and team alignment  [M01-M06] [Lead: IDE; 
Participants: All] The objective of this task is to establish a properly working framework in order to define a 
collaborative alliance of working groups (also called Integrated Project Delivery) to define partners' participation and 
responsibilities in each WP and to optimize project results in quality and time. The first step is to define the end users’ 
needs for the new bio-based products developed in this project. This will include chemical, physical, and mechanical 
properties needed for the new bio-based products. Then, to establishes the retrofit, changes and upgrades needed in 
the manufacture lines to reach GREEN -LOOP objectives. For each working group, a flowchart will be created to 
represent the team alignment and describe the activities to perform, as well as the data or materials needed per task. 
Task 1.3. Validation KPI’s definition  [M01-M08] [Lead: AIMEN; Participants: All] To describe the impacts 
achieved from the manufacture of novel bio-based materials in the value chains , a set of  relevant KPI’s will be 
identified. The KPI’s definition will include sustainability factors (usage of  raw/renewable materials, solid waste 
generation, recycling, reuse and refurbish), energy impacts (primary energy consumption, efficiency, potential energy 
savings), environmental burden from harmful substances (greenhouse emissions: CH4, CO2, pollutions). Also, some 
non-energy factors: industrial safety/risk,  economic factors (investment/operating cost) will be reviewed and 
quantified within the KPI’s. The KPI’s database will be created based on bio value chains, available sources, databases 
of materials (e.g. Materials Genome Initiative, AFLOW), including information from related EU projects (T7.5) 
Task 1.4. Inline monitoring and quality controls [M10-M36] [Lead: IRIS; Participants: IDE, AAU, MYX, 
LBRT, FHF, NCC, UBRIS, NIC] An ICT infrastructure to coordinate the data gathering will be developed by IRIS 
in this task. Firstly, manufacturers, supported by IRIS, will carried out the selection of the sensors to be installed from 
the technical requirements defined in Task 1.2. Then, ICT can be defined to interconnect all data from manufacture 
to the GREEN-LOOP platform for value chain optimization. The monitoring system will be designed to be part of 
the Internet of Things and it will be accessible online. This infrastructure will consist on: i) Systems for collection of 
data (monitoring, sensors, machine, humans, quality products). ii) System and protocols for communicating data. iii) 
Quality of the data. The prototype of ICT platform will be tested during lab trials and optimization of the processes, 
the final version will be deployed to complete the upscaling up to TRL6 in Task 6.1.

Objectives 
To create and utilize a methodology to obtain optimized designs, ensuring the circularity of bio -based materials. 
Reduce the environmental impact by implementing A.I models, thermo-economy studies and environmental analysis. 
Finally, implement a platform to evaluate and optimise the bio value chains. 
 
Description of work 
Task 2.1 Circular Economy, End of Life strategies and value chain assessment [M01-M15] [Lead: GIG; 
Participants: AXIA, AIMEN, MYX, LBRT, FHF, NCC, UBRIS, NIC , ZAG ]•Subtask 2.1.1 Assessment on 
Value chains (AXIA): For each of the bio -based materials, a map of the value chain will be created. The analysis 
will study the transformatio n from linear value chain  to circular approach, including resource optimisation, waste 
reduction, reuse, refurbish, recycle , circular business (T7.1.) and Blue economy impacts. Results will be inputs for 
environmental activities (T2.3) and platform (T2.5.) •Subtask 2.1.1 Circularity by design and end of life (EoL) 
strategies (GIG): Analysis of raw materials, assembly configurations and disassembly alternatives will be performed. 
Optimum scenarios will be defined to reach circularity by design  (made to be made again , T6.5). Results will be 
inputs of LCA/LCC analyses in T2.3. GIG will develop bill of materials and indicators to evaluate the circularity. 
Task 2.2 Energy and Exergy analysis of the systems [M07-M36] [Lead: IDE; Participants: MYX, LBRT, FHF, 
NCC, UBRIS, NIC] Energy transformations in quality and quantity are analysed for each material in two different 
stages of the product's life. First, at lab scale, and then during upscaling of the manufacture. Energy optimisation is 
done bas ed on: i) Identification  of irreversibilities ii) Understanding why energy losses occur. iii) Evaluation of 
changes, parameters, and configurations. iv) Improvement suggestions. v) Understanding the effects of losses and 
assessing the final cost of products. Results will feed T2.3 
 Task 2.3 Tools and methodology for LCA, LCC, and Social LCA assessments  [M07-M36] [Lead: AIMEN; 
Participants: AXIA, GIG, MYX, LBRT, FHF, NCC, UBRIS, NIC] LCA will be assessed mainly on recyclability, 
reuse, refurbish, waste reduction,  litter impact  and GHG emissions, complemented by other relevant impact 
categories. LCC will estimate capital, operational costs and cost reductions, and indirect costs related to 
environmental externalities, as well as and the asset's residual value at the end of its life. The S-LCA will be conducted 
for different social indicators related to impact categories . During the first stages, this task will be focused on raw 
materials and resources (WP3 to WP5). In validation phase, it will include the industrially relevant data (WP6). In 
both cases, data from energy studies (T2.2) and value chain assessment (T2.1) will be used. 
Task 2.4 Standardisation activities of GREEN-LOOP [M01-M36] [Lead: ASI; Participants: ISQ, MYX, 
LBRT, FHF, NCC, UBRIS, NIC] From the definition of the bio -based materials, the standardization potential of 
the end products will be investigated, allowing the project to interact with the related standardization technical 
committees. At first, an initial study of the standardization landscape will be performed, based on the needs of material 
developments (WP3 to WP5), regarding relevant existing standards (ISO, CEN, OSHA, ACEA, etc) and the related 
standardization committees (e.g. WRAP, ECN ). ASI together with other consortium members will develop a 
standardization plan detailing the relevant standardization activities and actions taken by the project.

in the GREEN-LOOP platform to boost value chains, social engagement and training activities (T2.1, T2.5 and T8.4).
