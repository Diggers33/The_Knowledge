3.2  Capacity of participants and consortium as a whole  
The RECREATE consortium consists of 21 partners from 9 different EU countries (Fig.17). Overall, 
the consortium is highly multidisciplinary and exhibits very complementary expertise and 
competences. The lead partner i s Politecnico di Milano (POLIMI), which is the 1 st public technical 
university in Italy, the 6 th in Europe in the area “Engineering” 
(https://www.topuniversities.com/universities/politecnico-di-milano). POLIMI will participate with 3 
different groups, namely the Department of Chemistry, Materials and Chemical Engineering “Giulio 
Natta” (https://www.cmic.polimi.it, with project coordinator Prof. G. Griffini, green solvolysis, smart 
resins), the Department of Mechanical Engineering (https://www.mecc.polimi.it, electrofragmentation, 
AI-based decision support systems), and METID (https://www.metid.polimi.it/), which will be 
engaged in the co-design and realization of innovative learning resources in collaboration with GRIFO, 
for the realization of MOOCs, serious gaming approaches to composite circularity education and digital 
twins (https://www.grifomultimedia.it/en/home-en/). POLIMI has a well-established and documented 
experience as participant and coordinator of EU-funded research projects, and researchers from the two 
Departments are commo nly engaged in several projects concerning circular economy, composite 
recycling, and smart polymers. Moreover, POLIMI will have a direct access to some of the European 
university networks POLIMI and the other academic partners are 
currently part of (e.g., ENHANCE, IDEA League, Alliance4Tech), for 
an efficient replication of the innovative learning tools developed in 
the project. The other research partners involved in RECREATE as

business cases by Giacomelli-Media (G_MEDIA), which possess relevant expertise in delivering

and remanufacturing (AVK and APRA Europe, respectively). G_MEDIA possess a vast and diverse 
network of industrial contacts including central-east Europe, and could engage stakeholders potentially 
very interested to the results of RECREATE project.  
Finally, dedicated competences in humanities and social sciences are guaranteed by the presence of 
METID in the Consortium, with the support of GRIFO Multimedia for gamification and realization 
of relevant digital tools, as well as by the activities undertaken by UPAT for the development of an 
integrated environmental, social and economic sustainability assessment. Dedicated activities 
concerning Open Sciences and Gender inclusion will be continuously monitored by POLIMI, which 
will be leading a task in WP9 (T9.4) fully dedicated to these aspects.
