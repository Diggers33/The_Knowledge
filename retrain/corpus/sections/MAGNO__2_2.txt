3.2.4. Industrial/commercial involvement to ensure exploitation of the results 
MAGNO is fortunate to draw on the experience of a team which includes IDE and IRIS (IT and informatics) supported 
by packaging experts (CETEC, FHF, DNV), recycling experts (EUPC, FHF, CETEC), environmental assessment 
professionals (UOB, INSTM), and sustainability and social activity practitioners (HOLOSS, KVC). The consortium 
will define strong circular economy business strategies to obtain industri al cooperation agreements with food multi-
actors. During this process, KVC and DVN will be responsible for ensuri ng that the Key Exploitable Results are 
protected during the project and that they are both protected and exploited after project completion.  
3.2.5. Other countries and international organisation 
The MAGNO consortium includes 2 partners (UOB - Lebanon, INSTM - Tunisia) and one third party (UOS -Tunisia) 
from non-EU countries. Tunisia and Lebanon are key countries in the fight on plastic pollution in Eur ope due to their 
location in the Mediterranean Sea basin, which suffers greatly from plastic pollution. To address EU objectives, it is 
important for these countries to develop effective waste management syste ms and for the international community to 
support them in their efforts. These partners provide research expertise and international insight into the problems facing 
plastic form food packaging. The researchers from UOB have participated in EU projects such as CLAIM17 (Cleaning 
Litter by developing and Applying Innovative Methods in European seas) and SWaTH125 (Sustainable Wastewater 
Treatment for Hospitals). INSTM has experience leading Mediterranean and African projects in the field of marine litter 
such as Plastic Buster CAP126 and Nutec Plastics 127. INSTM also has considerable working experience in European 
programs, with particular focus on plastic monitoring and cleaning as part of  CLAIM17. Last but not least, UOS 
predominantly holds experience under the EPLUS program via participation on projects such as Boosting Environmental 
Protection and Energy Efficient Buildings in Mediterranean Region 128 and Climate Change in Agriculture 129. In 
conclusion, the directly relevant experience that UOB, INSTM and UOS will bring to the project means their participation 
will add significant value  to the research effort. In specific, they will be responsible fo r conducting the following 
activities: i) Continuous support to the food value chain in terms of pollution; ii) Assessing the impact of litter in waters; 
and iii) Studying the impacts of plastics on human health and safety. Furthermore, the activities to be undertaken by 
partners from non-EU countries do not raise any potential ethical concerns and would be allowed in EU member states.
