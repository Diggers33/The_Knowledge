=== Domain B: photonic sensors for pharmaceutical inline process analytical technology (PAT) ===
=== Section 2_1: Expected outcomes and impacts ===

1.1. Expected impacts of the project

The expected impacts of the project are:

- Improved safety and reduced risks: the project will support and contribute to the implementation of the EU strategy on Chemical Safety that aims at minimising the risks related to the use of chemicals and to improve chemical safety for consumers. The project will contribute to the achievement of the relevant chemical safety roadmaps and policy objectives, such as "The Commission strategy for a non-toxic environment", or "The Chemicals Strategy for Sustainability". This will be achieved by the project through the design of new, more efficient and safer chemical and materials production processes.
- Reduced energy consumption: the project will aim at reducing the energy consumption in the production of chemicals and materials, as well as in the use and end of life of chemicals and materials, thereby contributing to the energy efficiency targets set by the Energy Union and the EU climate objectives.
- Improved resource efficiency: the project will contribute to the transition towards a more circular economy by improving the resource efficiency of the production processes and the use and end of life of chemicals and materials.
- Increased competitiveness and Innovation: the project will contribute to the objectives of the EU Industrial Policy, and specifically to the achievement of the key objectives of the Strategic Implementation Plan of the Photonics Public Private Partnership, by supporting the development of innovative photonics-based manufacturing processes for chemical and material production, which will increase the competitiveness of the EU industry and contribute to the achievement of the targets for the photonics sector.
- Societal challenges: the project will contribute to the achievement of the objectives of the policy areas addressed by the in topic, i.e. "Manufacturing technologies and advanced materials" and "Advanced manufacturing processes", and of the relevant roadmaps and strategies for the topics addressed by the project, through the support to the development of new, innovative and more efficient manufacturing processes and the development of new materials with improved properties for the manufacturing processes.
- Regulatory framework: the project will support the regulatory framework to ensure a high level of protection of human health and the environment by contributing to the implementation of the policy framework, including the Chemicals Strategy for Sustainability, the Commission strategy for a non-toxic environment, the EU Industrial Policy and the Strategic Implementation Plan of the Photonics Public Private Partnership, and the Clean Energy for All Europeans Package.
- Better regulation: the project will contribute to the objectives of the Better Regulation Policy by ensuring that the policy framework is evidence-based and fit for purpose, by supporting the implementation and monitoring of the policy framework and by contributing to the achievement of the objectives of the policy framework.
- Economic growth and jobs: the project will contribute to the creation of new jobs and the growth of the EU economy by supporting the development of new, innovative and more efficient manufacturing processes and the development of new materials with improved properties for the manufacturing processes.
- Sustainable development: the project will contribute to the achievement of the objectives of the EU Sustainable Development Strategy by supporting the development of new, innovative and more efficient manufacturing processes and the development of new materials with improved properties for the manufacturing processes.
- International cooperation: the project will contribute to the achievement of the objectives of the EU international cooperation strategy by supporting the development of new, innovative and more efficient manufacturing processes and the development of new materials with improved properties for the manufacturing processes.
- Relevant to this topic: the project will contribute to the achievement of the objectives of the topic by supporting the development of new, innovative and more efficient manufacturing processes and the development of new materials with improved properties for the manufacturing processes.

1.2. Innovation, market and economic potential

The expected high impact of the project is based on a consolidated state-of-the-art and the existence of a real market: the chemical and material production industry is a key sector of the EU economy, with a turnover of more than 600 billion € (including 21% of the turnover of the EU chemical industry and 31% of the turnover of the EU plastic industry) and a number of companies of more than 2 million (including 33% of the EU chemical industry and 62% of the EU plastic industry). The sector is very important in terms of employment, with 2.3 million (5.2% of the EU workforce) direct jobs and 11 million indirect jobs. It is a key sector for the EU economy and a key sector for the EU industry, with a strong impact on the competitiveness of the EU industry.

The project will develop new, innovative and more efficient manufacturing processes for chemical and material production, which will be based on new, innovative and more efficient photonics-based manufacturing technologies. The new manufacturing processes will be developed for the production of new, innovative and more efficient materials, which will be used in the manufacturing processes and in the end products. The project will contribute to the achievement of the objectives of the EU Industrial Policy and specifically of the Strategic Implementation Plan of the Photonics Public Private Partnership by supporting the development of innovative photonics-based manufacturing processes for chemical and material production
