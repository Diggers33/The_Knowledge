2.3 Summary  KEY ELEMENT OF THE IMPACT SECTION 
   
SPECIFIC NEEDS What are the specific needs that triggered this project?  Need to mitigate the low quality of water sources due to contamination of soil and freshwater   Need to foster the protection of the drinking water chain in regard to DBPs produced when chlorine disinfectant reacts with organic matter in water    Need to expand a limited knowledge on the toxicity and impact of DBPs on the environment and human health     Need to mitigate the impacts of pathogen development in water sources and lake eutrophication (linked to increased algal blooms) resulting from climate change    Need to build society and governments’ awareness of drinking water contaminants.   
D & E & C MEASURES What dissemination, exploitation and communication measures will you apply to the results?  Dissemination:  Via one opening dissemination events organization of an awareness raising webinar series, development of two-creation workshops, organization of one final dissemination event, development of partner’s individual dissemination plans, participation in 10 third party events and 40 conferences.  Exploitation:   Via definition of key exploitable results (KERs), development of strategy for intellectual property rights (IPR) management, cooperation with water-oriented Living Labs (WoLLs), participation in Water Europe’s exploitation-oriented events, organisation of one focus group to assess market gaps and possibilities for long-term exploitation of the project’s final results, and 20 open-source scientific publications    Communication:  Via project website, in-hand dissemination tools (such as brochures, flyers, roll-ups and other educational materials), online dissemination tools (such as e-newsletter, educational visual materials, videos, press releases) and seeking complementarities and synergies with other relevant EU-funded projects and actions   
EXPECTED RESULTS What do you expect to generate by the end of the project?  KER1: Sensors and sensing infrastructures for the identification of DBPs and/or the main materials they interact with (TG2, TG3)  KER1: Cost-effective treatment technologies for drinking water (TG2, TG3, TG4)  KER2: Real-time interactive maps of DBPs from sources to supply (TG1, TG2, TG3, TG4)   KER2: Central Knowledge Base where information about DBPs can be added worldwide, associating this information with space and time, allowing the development of web-based data geo-visualisation (TG1, TG2, TG3, TG4)  KER3: Advanced and cost-effective alternative disinfection processes to prevent/minimize DBPs production (TG2, TG3)  KER4: Recommendations on Prevention Measures for Safety Planning (TG1, TG2, TG3, TG4, TG5)  KER4: Indices for drinking water safety plan (TG1, TG2, TG3, TG4, TG5)

 TARGET GROUPS Who will use or further up-take the results of the project? Who will benefit from the results of the project?   TG1: Higher education institutions, research organisations and research networks connected to water issues;     TG2: Municipal and private water utility organisations, water treatment companies, water supplier companies, water operators and water regulators;    TG3: National & European policy makers (municipalities, inter-governmental bodies, EU agencies, etc.), particularly those related to water safety policies;    TG4: Community groups, NGOs, professional associations, foundations and citizens;    TG5: Mass media, online news outlets    
IMPACTS What are the expected wider scientific, economic and societal effects of the project contributing to the expected impacts outlined in the respective destination in the work programme? Scientific: Increased understanding of water pollutants and development of new technologies, sensing systems, monitoring infrastructures, drinking water disinfection and water treatment methodologies, satellite work and new algorithms.  Scientific innovation will render 20 peer reviewed scientific publications as well as participation in 40 international conferences. 8 PhD and 18 MSc students will be trained in the context of the project, and the project will be included in 4 curricular units at pre- and post-graduation levels. Economic: 4 potential innovative technologies/patents and IPR (based on the novel sensing and monitoring system as well as advanced disinfection and water treatment technologies to be developed) and implementation of novel raw water treatments able to remove DBPs and algal blooms precursors which will reduce drinking water disinfection costs by at least 5%. Involvement of other utility companies to spread the integrated monitoring of the water quality using WE network.  Societal:  Enabling the implementation of an integrated monitoring solution that ensures water quality for safe human use across the EU and the integration of the new understanding on prevention and mitigation approaches to DBPs in drinking water at the legislative level. The preventive measures to protect drinking water will target water distributors, stakeholders and consumers which will raise societal awareness and understanding on DBPs. The results will contribute to policy-making through norms, standards and regulation (WP6) in Europe in drinking water and water sources management  
OUTCOMES What change do you expect to see after successful dissemination and exploitation of project results to the target group(s)?  Better understanding surrounding DBPs formation and transmission pathways which will allow the establishment of adequate measures to prevent/minimize their production, as well as mitigate their impacts (EO1, EO2)    New disinfection technologies will lead to predictions of DBPs’ ecological and human health impact based on silico QSAR and MCDM, and advanced and cost-effective water quality sensors will provide real time information on drinking water quality from sources to supply (EO3, EO4, EO5), delivering ready-made outputs for decision-making and consideration in future legislation    A central knowledge base with information about DBPs will be fed by authorized users. Alternative drinking water technologies involving MP UV radiation will be targeted in order to reduce the need for chlorination leading to lower levels of DBPs in drinking water, and innovative and cost-effective treatment approaches involving adsorption and advanced oxidation will be developed to remove contaminants from water (EO6, EO7, EO8)    Prevention plans to protect drinking water from the source to the supply will be developed, and project results will be published in an accessible and comprehensive way to the public (EO9, EO10, EO11), spreading the use of the advanced drinking water treatment and disinfection processes and technologies developed.
