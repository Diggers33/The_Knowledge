=== Domain B: photonic sensors for pharmaceutical inline process analytical technology (PAT) ===
=== Section 1_4: Ambition and innovation beyond the state of the art ===

1.4.1 The current state of the art

Our technology is based on our expertise in photonic sensors and in particular in near-infrared (NIR) spectroscopy. Our most relevant results in this context are:

- A Photonic NIR spectrometer for the measurements of aqueous solutions, such as water-alcohol mixtures. We designed a NIR spectrometer based on photonic components fabricated in our foundry and we demonstrated that it can measure water-alcohol mixtures with very high sensitivity and reproducibility. We also demonstrated that our NIR spectrometer can be used for machine learning models for the analysis of these mixtures (see [1]).

- An optofluidic NIR sensor for the inline analysis of liquid mixtures. We designed an optofluidic NIR sensor based on photonic components fabricated in our foundry and we demonstrated that it can measure water-acetone mixtures with very high sensitivity and reproducibility. We also demonstrated that our NIR spectrometer can be used for machine learning models for the analysis of these mixtures (see [2]).

- A photonic NIR sensor for the detection of ketones in alcoholic beverages. We designed a photonic NIR sensor based on photonic components fabricated in our foundry and we demonstrated that it can measure ketones in alcoholic beverages such as whiskey and rum with very high sensitivity and reproducibility. We also demonstrated that our NIR spectrometer can be used for machine learning models for the analysis of these mixtures. We demonstrated that our NIR system is very sensitive to the presence of ketones (see [3]).
