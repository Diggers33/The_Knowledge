=== Domain C: hyperspectral imaging for automated plastic waste sorting in circular economy facilities ===
=== Section 2_3: Communication and open science ===

1.1. Impact of the project  
The HYPERSPECTRUM project will deliver new hyperspectral imaging technologies that will empower the circular economy of plastic waste.  
The new hyperspectral imaging technologies will be deployed to circular economy facilities to enable automated plastic waste sorting, which will increase their efficiency by 20% (compared to current manual sorting) and reduce their operation costs by 15%. Furthermore, the new sorting technologies will increase the recycled plastic quality by 10%.  
The HYPERSPECTRUM project will contribute to the creation of new EU companies (5) and new jobs (around 50) directly linked to the manufacturing and deployment of the new hyperspectral imaging systems. Furthermore, the project will create new jobs (around 200) in circular economy facilities due to the increased waste processing.  
The HYPERSPECTRUM project will contribute to the sustainability of the EU by reducing the amount of plastic waste sent to landfills and incinerators, which will have a positive impact on the circular economy of plastic waste and will create a cleaner and healthier environment.  
1.2. Measures to maximise impact  
1.2.1. Dissemination and exploitation of results  
We will continuously monitor the dissemination and exploitation activities to ensure the maximum impact of the project. We will submit the results of our research to scientific journals and present them at conferences to engage with the scientific community. Our involvement in the Photonic21 association will help us reach a broader audience. Furthermore, we will exploit the results of the project through the commercialization of our new hyperspectral imaging systems.  
1.2.2. Communication activities  
We will implement a communication plan to increase awareness and understanding of the project among the general public. The plan will include the creation of a user-friendly website, the publication of articles in newspapers and magazines, the organization of workshops and demonstrations in circular economy facilities, and the participation in local and international events. The website will be updated regularly with the results of our research and the exploitation of the project.  
1.3. Summary of the project’s deliverables and milestones  
The project will generate 30 deliverables and 36 milestones. The most important deliverables are: D3.2 (demonstrator #1), D3.3 (demonstrator #2), D3.4 (demonstrator #3), and D4.3 (commercial hyperspectral imaging system). The most important milestones are: MS1 (data gathering completed), MS12 (demonstrator #1 completed), MS13 (demonstrator #2 completed), and MS14 (demonstrator #3 completed).  
1.4. Summary of the costs and the funding required for the project  
The total cost of the project is 3,063,672.02 €. The requested EU contribution is 2,999,820.62 €. The total person-months required for the project are 28.9, 13.0, 12.9, 13.4, 13.0, 12.9, and 12.9 for WP1, WP2, WP3, WP4, WP5, WP6, and WP7, respectively.  
The HYPERSPECTRUM project has a 36-month duration and was submitted to the NMBP-22-2020 call.
