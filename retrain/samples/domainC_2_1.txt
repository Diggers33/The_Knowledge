=== Section 2_1: Expected outcomes and impacts ===

1.1. Expected impacts

The impacts relevant to the proposed project and linked to the topics addressed in the call text are summarised in the table below. In the table, the “H” means High impact, “M” means Medium impact, “L” means Low impact, and “-” means no impact. The letter in brackets indicates our level of contribution to the impact (H, M or L). The impacts shown in the table are also included in the proposal Impact section.

Table 1: Impact table summarising the expected contribution of the proposed project activities to the Call Impact areas

Expected impacts	Expected impact addressed by the topic How our proposed project addresses the impact
1. Development of new and more efficient and sustainable technologies, processes and services (DEPLOY-1-2018-2019) Innovation in the field of advanced manufacturing processes, materials and product design is essential for the competitiveness of the European manufacturing industry. The objective of this topic is to develop and demonstrate novel and efficient manufacturing processes and systems to improve the competitiveness of EU manufacturing industry. We will develop and demonstrate a novel and efficient manufacturing process for the production of plastic films using a novel Solvent Casting process. This will reduce the production time of plastic films by a factor of 3, while maintaining the same quality, and the energy used by a factor of 10 compared to the existing technologies. In addition, we will produce the films using 100% biobased materials, contributing to the sustainability of the process. Our solution will be validated by the industrial end-user and in an industrial environment, ensuring a smooth market uptake.
We will also develop new sensing, feedback and control technologies for the Solvent Casting process, that will allow to adapt the process to the characteristics of the film to be produced.
2. New and improved innovative and sustainable materials (materials with better performance and/or lower environmental footprint) (DEPLOY-1-2018-2019) We will develop novel biobased materials for food packaging. These materials will be the Polyhydroxyalkanoates PHBV and PHBH. These materials will be produced in the form of thin films to be used as food packaging. The films will be produced by a Solvent Casting process, which will allow to obtain films with a thickness of 50 microns. We will demonstrate how these films can be used for food packaging, where their biodegradability will be a major advantage. We will demonstrate the films in 3 different food packaging applications to ensure a wide applicability. We will demonstrate our films for food packaging in an industrial environment. This will ensure a smooth market uptake.
