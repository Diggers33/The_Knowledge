Project title 3 Innovative manufacturing processes and in-line monitoring techniques for the OLED
and thin film and organic photovoltaic industries (CIGS and OPV)

Free keywords

Abstract 7
Opto-electronic devices are opening exciting new applications everyday. With new display options using pliable
substrates such as plastic and flexible glass, OLEDs manufacturers are bringing a wide range of new applications
in lighting (e.g. energy efficient lighting) and different type of displays. Similarly, with the emergence of thin-film
technologies in the solar cells market, new applications ranging from installations on curved surfaces to building-
integrated PV has become possible. However, to meet the industry requirements for mass production, including low
cost, manufacturing volumes and efficiency, many challenges still need to be addressed. These challenges for OPV,
OLED and CIGs are scale-up from laboratory to mass production, selection of efficient manufacturing processes,
employing inspection, control and measurement techniques to improve yield, quality and time-to-market.
OLEDSOLAR aims to tackle these challenges by developinginnovative manufacturing processes for critical
steps in the production of opto-electronic devices including OLEDs, OPVs and CIGs solar cells. The proposed
activities include reconfigurable high yield (>10% improvement) processes to be scaled up, tested at pilot lines and
implemented in production line for validation. A complete system of inspection, quality control, functional testing
and measurements using advance system and sensors will be optimised in the project for efficient manufacturing of
opto-electronics parts. Recycling and re-use strategies will be developed allowing resource efficiency and reduction
of high value product wastes. Automation and advance processing software will be developed for overall control and
monitoring of roll-to-roll (R2R) and sheet-to-sheet (S2S) manufacturing process.
During 36 months, a multidisciplinary team of leading RTOs and industries in this field will dedicate their resources
and effort to perform proposed activities in 8 WPs and guarantee the maximum impact of OLEDSOLAR project.

Objectives
To develop specification and requirements of manufacturing, inspection, quality control, automation and recycling.
Description of work and role of partners
WP1 - Identification of opto-electrical components manufacturing and inspection requirements at production
scale [Months: 1-4]
ASPF, VTT, Fraunhofer, TNO, IRIS, TWI, Solibro, BUL, LEITAT, Coatema, DTF, SAL, Advent, INURU, SMIT
Description of work
T1.1 Identification of OLEDs manufacturing requirements (Lead: INURU) In this task OLEDs manufacturing
requirements will be analysed for rigid displays and flexible displays application. The need of process optimisation in
S2S manufacturing as well as implementation in roll to roll manufacturing process will be established.
T1.2 Identification of solar cells manufacturing requirements (Lead: OPVIUS, partner: Solibro) In this task
manufacturing process factors for yield improvement, improvement of throughput, and reduction of waste will be
analysed for CIGS and OPVs. Different existing market solutions will be analysed and potential and benefits of
proposed solution will be compared. The end users’ product and production requirements will be specified as well. The
requirements/specifications in general are identified which can then serve as a benchmark for the assessment of the
current production process in T1.3.
T1.3 Assessment of current production processes and process tools adaptability as per production requirements (Lead:
Smit TS, Partner: Coatema, OPVIUS, Solibro, INURU) In this tasks assessment of current production processes of
OLEDs, OPVs and CIGs will be analysed and adaptability of tools as per change of demand or change of product will
be studied.
T1.4 Establishing requirements for inspection, quality and process control (Lead: SOLIBRO, Partners: OPVIUS, Smit
TS, DTF, INURU, CTR, DTF, FhG, Coatema) In this task metrology, in-line inspection and measurement techniques
requirements will be established for OLEDs, OPVs, and CIGs manufacturing in S2S and roll to roll manufacturing
process.
T1.5 Analysis of material challenges for manufacturing process reconfiguration and recyclability (Lead: Advent,
Partners: LEITAT, VTT, Smit TS, DTF) Material challenges will be analysed from manufacturing reconfigurability,
material transport and handling and recycling issues in production lines. Advent will investigate highly efficient organic
materials in order to tweak the material properties towards a simultaneous improvement of synthesis scalability. This
will be accomplished by the synergistic application of continuous flow synthesis and traditional organic synthesis
reactors. Continuous flow methods are employed for the controlled polymerization of the state of the art polymeric
semiconductors including optimization and upscaling experiments. Furthermore, eco-friendly ink formulations will be
developed for the efficient printing and/or coating of large area processing.

Work package number 9 WP2 Lead beneficiary 10 3 - TNO
Work package title Development of innovative manufacturing processes for OLEDs and solar cells
Start month 4 End month 34
Objectives
To develop and optimize innovative deposition, packaging and patterning techniques for high volume manufacturing
with regard to product quality and yield and industrial scaled up implementation.This concerns spatial atomic layer
(inkjet, screen and gravure) printing, slot-die coating and lamination, PECVD, evaporation and sputtering deposition
techniques for adding barrier, front window, packaging and encapsulation functionality.
The resulting techniques will be used at pilot scale for demonstration and validation activities in and in conjunction
with production lines of end user partners in WP6.
Description of work and role of partners
WP2 - Development of innovative manufacturing processes for OLEDs and solar cells [Months: 4-34]
TNO, VTT, Fraunhofer, IRIS, ASPF, CSEM, Solibro, Coatema, DTF, Advent, INURU, SMIT
T2.1 Developing adaptive material handling system for R2R and S2S production line (Lead: Coatema, Partner:
DTF, VTT) In this task substrate material handling system will be developed and tested in order to increase the
registration accuracy and the substrate dimensional stability. Intra equipment automation and control via central decision
management software will be developed in this task as well.
T2.2 Optimisation of scalable manufacturing processes for solar cells and OLEDs (Lead: VTT, Partner: ASPF, Inuru,
Solibro) This task is based on the requirements of the industrial production lines at ASPF (OPV), Solibro (CIGS) and
Inuru (OLED) and focuses on optimizing those printing/coating processes (OPV/OLED) and studying if e.g. inkjet
printing processes can be utilized in CIGS production. ASPF uses a R2R slot die coating process, combined with laser
structuring and screen printing to prepare free-form OPV devices. The OLED process at Inuru is based on inkjet printing.
Solibro has a glass-glass process for producing CIGS modules. The bottle-necks and development needs of those
processes will be clarified, and solutions will be searched together with VTT. For OPVs, VTT can offer the R2R pilot
lines for testing e.g. screen printing of Ag electrodes with several different screen types to optimize the printing quality/
resolution, together with high registration accuracy (50 µm) to minimize waste and number of misaligned components.
ASPF will also focus on developing a post-patterning process, i.e. performing all laser steps after all layers have been
coated. The developed OPV process will be validated in T2.7/WP6 with ASPF for automation of the selected process
steps and feasibility in OPV production.
For OLEDs and CIGSs, VTT can offer S2S scale inkjet printing facilities with industrial scale print heads. The
development needs will be collected from Inuru and Solibro and solutions are looked for in the S2S test setup.The OLED
printing will be finally validated at Inuru’s production lines in T2.7/WP6 and VTT’s devices can be used to demonstrate
the possible utilization of inkjet in CIGS modules.
T2.3 Optimisation of R2R and S2S atomic layer deposition techniques and processes in R2R and S2S production process
(Lead: TNO, Partner: Smit TS, VTT, DTF, FhG; M3 – M28) In this task spatial atomic layer deposition technique will
be optimised, tested and scaled up. Carrier application is that of Al2O3 barrier coatings on PET, supplied by DTF. In
order to upscale the process for mass production, the lowest requirements will be identified in terms of temperature and
speed deposition as well as defect presence in correlation with the barrier properties on lab-scale and R2R tool (in static
mode) at TNO. While reducing the thermal budget of the s-ALD processes generally lower the quality barrier properties,
the use of atmospheric plasma enhancement (PE) technology will be explored to provide even better performance in
terms of WVTR barrier properties. Test will be carried out on lab scale and S2S tool with PE deposition at TNO to
determine effect on thermal budget and allowable deposition speed. Investigated will be by TNO, Smit TS the integration
options for in-line inspection of pre-deposition and post deposition coated foil such as particle detection, ellipsometry,
Raman, PL for automatic adjustment of process settings (for enhanced process control) Being the barrier (and potential
other coatings) applied for flexible and rolled applications, the barrier needs to withstand mechanical stress without
failures. Developed will be by Smit TS the use of organic pre-coating and post-coating respectively for planarization
and mechanical protection purposes as well as use of PET foil supplied with PSC layer from DTF with as aim a WVTR
better than 0.5 10-4 g /m2 d. The above will be used to prepare a Customer Requirement Specification (CRS) which will
detail the intended layer, the parameter ranges to enable PE s-ALD and the allocation slots for in-line characterization
in all relevant disciplines (mechanical, electrical, pneumatic and software) for a R2R-ALD tool.
Page 13 of 45
Smit TS will build a test bench which will be a loop-coater with three segments (set of deposition slots) of a R2R-
ALD drum. The test bench will make possible coating of foil in R2R mode with and without PE for analysis of layer
functionality and product compatibility (half-fabricates from partners). The tool is meant to be compatible with an
existing R2R sALD pilot production system to allow for end-users to select such a system for a (future) pilot line
and suitable for installing and/or interacting with in-line inspection of coatings such as ellipsometry, Raman, PL for
automatic adjustment of process settings (for enhanced process control), and to be available for demonstration in WP6.
In addition, Fraunhofer will develop their R2R evaporation process for OLEDs and provides also OLED samples/rolls
for testing the different encapsulation processes (WP2: inkjet/ALD/lamination and their combinations) and to develop
the R2R electrical characterization setup (WP3).
T2.4 Development of inkjet-based deposition technique for pre-encapsulation of solar cells and flexible OLEDs (Lead:
CSEM, Partner: VTT, Smit TS, INURU) Inkjet based deposition techniques for pre-encapsulation will be developed
and tested for R2R and S2S production of OLEDs, OPVs and CIGSs by CSEM where pilot testing will be done in
S2S setup (cleanroom environment, in air). R2R Scale up strategy for TRL9 system will be done by Smit TS prior
commercialisation. Adhesives that require a nitrogen environment, may be tested with the automated syringe dispensing
and UV curing platform (S2S, up to 5-inch square substrates) at CSEM. VTT will provide access to industrial scale print
heads in S2S scale from MBNL, which will allow transferring the process to pilot scale. The inkjet-based encapsulation
will be compared to other encapsulation techniques as such (for low WVTR requirements) and as a pre-encapsulation
technique (for high WVTR requirements) where inkjet is combined with ALD (T2.3) or barrier film lamination (T2.5).
T2.5 Development of reconfigurable and scalable lamination, joining and packaging process for OLEDs and solar cells
(Lead: VTT, Partner: CSEM, ASPF, Solibro) Different lamination, packaging and coating technologies will be optimised
in this task by VTT for efficient, high quality devices fabrication. Support from CSEM by testing new materials for the
lamination process, validating the quality of the encapsulation using accelerated life time and mechanical stress testing,
and performing WVTR measurements. Feasibility studies (CSEM) of the fabrication of optical micro/nano structures
for in/out coupling directly on the barrier or substrates. Materials for R2R lamination test in air at VTT will be selected
based on S2S tests at CSEM. ASPF will work on validating further automatization of its encapsulation (via lamination)
and finishing processes, using materials that have shown the best potential at VTT and CSEM. Different methods and
materials for joining/contacts will be tested and produced at VTT and their effect on lifetime and mechanical stability
will be tested at CSEM.
T2.6 Testing and validation of on production pilot line for roll-to-roll fabricated barrier films (LEAD: Fraunhofer,
Partner: TNO, Smit TS) Validation of defect free winding and web handling for barrier films with and without protective
liner films.
• Validation of the drying kinetics for sufficient dry and suitable pre-treatment (plasma process and cleaning) steps for
conditioning the substrates. Especially, an efficient substrate drying process is needed for a roll-to-roll manufacturing
process.
• Determination of substrate surface quality regarding defect level and surface topography of roll materials.
• Substrate packaging and shipment concepts (air-tight packaging to minimize moisture ingress during transportation
especially for roll materials)
T2.7 Systems level Testing and validation on production pilot lines (Lead: VTT, Partner: Fraunhofer, CSEM, Smit TS,
TNO, ASPF, SOLIBRO, Advent) In this task developed handling and printing techniques in T2.2, T2.4 and T2.5 will be
fitted in the production pilot lines of OLEDs, OPVs and CIGSs at VTT (R2R and S2S), TNO (R2R and S2S), Fraunhofer
and CSEM (S2S), SOLIBRO (S2S) and ASPF (R2R) and Smit TS (R2R) pilot lines. The S2S environment (single and

T3.1 Development of in-line optical inspection system for component level testing at production scale (Lead: IRIS,
Partner: CSEM, Fraunhofer, DTF) Different optical systems, based on both spectral reflectance and spectroscopic
imaging ellipsometry will be delivered for high speed inline monitoring of, on the one hand, the coating application
processes (range 1-50 microns dry) and, on the other hand, to assess the quality of organic deposits (range expected
50-200 nm dry) on a range of substrates. The selected optical systems, described below, will be assembled and pre-
calibrated in the lab scale on a relevant set of samples for obtaining respectively coatings and deposition in non-
continuous modes. It is envisaged that at least 3 systems will be placed along the film width to allow getting an idea
of the variation of the thickness. The thickness of the samples will be tested by TEM and the coatings through other
conventional gages to calibrate the optical systems. The measurement system will be evaluated for Repeatability and
Reproducibility using ANOVA gauge R&R system. CSEM supports with: 1) Evaluate the potential of OCT (optical
coherence tomography) as technique for inline inspection and position it as a benchmark to the inspection methods
developed in the project 2) Off-line inspection for referencing and calibration of the optical inline systems by SEM/
AFM/OCT, 3) Testing of inspection tools in S2S fabrication environment.
T3.2 Implementation and optimisation of NDT testing for zero-defect testing at device level for OLEDs and solar cells
(Lead: TWI, Partner: VTT) In this task, advanced NDT techniques will be developed to be deployed in a production
line with S2S or roll to roll environment. These techniques include shearography, thermography and other optical
techniques. Digital Shearography is an optical measurement technique for NDT and quality control which has found
wide industrial application especially for composite materials testing. It detects sub-surface defects (such as voids, dis-
bonds, delamination) within an object by identifying defect-induced deformation anomalies as depicted by shearography
fringe patterns. When there is no defect in the material, the fringe pattern is smooth or uniform. Otherwise, the fringe
pattern will be distorted. The commonly used means of stressing are vacuum pressure (exerted by a vacuum hood or
a vacuum chamber), thermal heating and vibration excitation. The application of shearography in this project will be
focused on pinhole detection in CIGS solar panels and OLED sheet. Since the pinholes are very small in size (less than
1mm), efforts will be made to find appropriate stressing methods such that the thin plate or film under inspection can
have sufficient deformation for the pinholes to be revealed.
For thermography, dark and illuminated lock-in thermography (LIT) will be optimised and implemented by TWI, and
finally tested at VTT’s R2R electrical measurement setup line.
The lock-in thermography is a non-destructive characterization method, which allows for high-resolution, noise reduced,
thermographic imaging for failure analysis based on imaging and localization of electrical defects. Analysis of hot spot,
electrical shunt defects and thermal discontinuities. For instance, any disruption of the p-n junction due to cracks and
scratches that occur during the processing of a solar cell may produce failures, which may lead to unwanted leakage
current (i.e. process-induced shunts). Due to the heat dissipation, these shunts can be detected thermographically.
Different modes of LIT to be implemented in OLEDSOLAR:
1. Dark LIT methods:
o Pulsed voltage is applied to the solar cell without illuminating it. It allows the detection of all types of shunts (e.g.
linear and non-linear shunts);
2. Illuminated LIT methods:
o Performed by illuminating the solar cell with pulsed light. With this technique, different common sources of
shunting in standard industrial-type solar cells can be detected (e.g. points like shunts, cracks, poor edge isolation,
contaminations). Spatial and time resolution enables use for inline characterization.
T3.3 Development of in-line Raman spectroscopy and luminescence testing processes and in-line prototype devices
(Lead: CTR, Partner: Solibro) This task will first establish the feasibility of non-destructive photonic in-line testing
techniques for the applications and needs defined in T1.4. In a second step, suitable in-line system demonstrators
Page 17 of 45
for deployment in relevant manufacturing settings will be developed. This will involve in particular two fundamental
techniques, (process) Raman spectroscopy and luminescence imaging. Raman spectroscopy: As a fully spectroscopic
technique, Raman spectroscopy is capable of non-invasive, contact-free qualitative and quantitative analysis of a wide
range of substance of direct relevance to the project. As a first step, the basic feasibility of the process analysis tasks
defined in T1.4 will be investigated under controlled laboratory conditions using a high-end multi-purpose Raman
spectrometer. This will involve establishing whether and how e.g. thin film thicknesses and composition (different
materials, doping levels etc.) of one or more components within OLED, CIGS and OPV manufacturing can be reliably
detected. In the second phase, selected of these approaches will be refined to be applicable in the process, i.e. be robust
enough for in-line use (exploiting e.g. self-referencing etc.) and transferred to suitable designed and adapted test units
for testing within T3.7.
Luminescence: Techniques to permit in-line characterization during various stages of solar cell manufacturing provide a
convenient means for optimizing yield, cost, and efficiency. In particular photo-luminescence measurements are widely
used for material characterization. In this project photoluminescence excitation spectroscopy (PLE), in which the steady-
state photoluminescence is monitored as a function of the wavelength of the incident illumination, will be implemented.
The method is also of high interest to CIGS deposition process control to ensure the optical & electrical quality of the
absorber layer, get a faster feedback during the introduction of new processes & materials and improve the yield. The use
of spectral and/or transient photoluminescence has shown promise on laboratory scale to supply valuable information
about the recombination properties of the absorber and its interfaces enabling a forward calculation of the Voc. The
proposed inline PL systems will be used to investigate the correlation of the absorber quality with its properties under
field conditions. The optimum PL characterization method and position within the process chain should be identified,
which enables the best prediction of the field properties based on inline measurement.
TNO, SAL, FhG-IWS and Brunel will work together on using above given optical measuring techniques as well as
the HSI technique for in-line quality control of plates with CIGS PV cell layer stacks, i.e. to identify (areas with) a
low quality absorber layer that would lead to poor cell performance. TNO will produce series of semi-fabricates on
glass and/or steel plates on their batch-production pilot line(s) at Solliance for which the performance and properties
will be varied (by changing Cu, In and Ga ratios and layers’ thickness). The plates will be sent to SAL for Raman and
luminescent measurements to FhG-IWS for (VNIR and laser) HSI measurements) and/or finished into working devices
and their performance determined at TNO.
Brunel will train predictive models to link the luminescent image data with the cell performance parameters (particularly
Voc) and possibly also with shunts between back and front electrodes. Similarly FHG-IWS will use the hyper spectral
image data sets for training and validating models for predicting the Raman and luminescent measurement and the cell
performance.
T3.4 Qualification of thin film barrier coatings for flexible devices (Lead: Fraunhofer, Partner: CSEM, Smit TS, INURU)
• Water vapour transmission rate (WVTR) analysis of gas barrier films down to 10-6 g/m²*d for substrates and top
encapsulations. Additionally, the WVTR measurements will be correlated by extrinsic degradation evaluations applying
large area optical calcium tests. The Ca will be deposited under vacuum condition in Roll-to-Roll (300 mm width and
several meters long). The coated and encapsulated Ca- samples will be stored under different climate condition up to
85°C/85% r.h. The degradation, like side ingress and dark spot nucleation will be quantified by image processing of
recorded Ca sample images by high resolution. The pin hole density will be determined over large area of 10 x 10 cm²
and will be correlated with the WVTR measurement under steady state mode.
• Validation of the pin hole density determination on 10 x 10 cm² OLED devices on barrier films to quantify the dark
spot nucleation and side ingress behaviour.
• Determination of leak times, if desiccants with water trap functionality are implemented inside the devices.
• Defect image analysis recorded by hyperspectral imaging (HSI). HSI will be applied on barrier films, calcium
test samples and flexible organic devices. The defect maps are correlated directly with pin hole densities, layer de-
laminations and electro-optical behaviour. Afterwards, the devices will be stored under climate conditions and in shelves
for operational long-time tests. All types of investigations will be correlated for rapid test inspections using HSI.
• Analysis of aging effects and device performance using hyperspectral imaging (HSI) to develop derive specific
algorithms for an inline inspection system to identify just in time yield relevant defects.
• the measurement and quantification of the WVTR of ultra-barriers using the optical Ca test in S2S environment by
CSEM, mapping of the water ingress.
T3.5 Large area printing quality measurement and comparison to design layout (Lead: VTT, Partner: ASPF, Coatema) A
camera for high-resolution / micro scale mapping of the large printing area (one repeat unit) will be developed to follow
printing defects. The image will be automatically compared with the printing design layout, defects will be marked,
and errors highlighted in pictures will be shown to the printing machine operator. The error data with positions will
be stored in cloud for the consequent process phases. The information is also possible to combine with R2R electrical
characterization data. Printing quality measurements will be carried out by scalable 100%-micron scale inspection of
the printed layers to R2R-produced printed electronics with high resolution (>10k pixel) line camera, high power led
Page 18 of 45
illumination and ink-jet marking. This measurement system stores faulty printed position to file / cloud for the usage
at the later process phases. Second part of the system is inkjet-printer which marks every printed repeat length of the
printed roll so that faulty printed devices can be passed by combining repeat length info with high resolution line camera
inspection result. Inspection is done by machine vision algorithms by comparing camera image to the design layout
and differences will be highlighted for the printing machine operator so that the operator can optimize printing machine
print units. The inspection data is also stored for consequent process phases to avoid further processing with non-
working printed devices in expensive process phases. This scalable inspection system gives possibility to monitor 100%
of the high volume printed electronics R2R-production. This will improve yield and prevent large amount of scrap in
production, which is very important especially in case of the high-cost post-processing phases (add-on components and
system level packaging). This in-line camera system development also expands machine vision in printing machines to
micron scale detection at the fast-moving web by new kind of pulsed high power led illumination light source.
T3.6 Machine vision based micro meter level register accuracy measurement for visible and highly transparent layers
(Lead: VTT, Partner: ASPF, Coatema) In this task, VTT will develop a camera system, which can detect the printed
patterns with µm-scale resolution, even with transparent inks. VTT already has previous experience about similar
cameras / machine vision-based measurements and about transparent material registration. This experience is utilized in
the implementation of the task. Different printing layers of printed electronics are very different from each other. Some
are opaque, and some are transparent. Some of them are so transparent that they can not necessarily even be seen by the
naked eye in a stationary specimen. This will be even more challenging on the moving line. In practice, these different
layers of materials require a different illumination geometry in the used camera system (up to 3 different illuminations).
In this case, VTT’s cheap LED technology is a natural solution taking into consideration that the minimum requirement
is that one camera per printing unit is used in the production and one printing machine consists of several printing
units. Coatema will give outlook for registration automation concept and process control. This is a study about future
integration and upscaling. This is paper work based on experimental results from VTT, Coatema.
T3.7 Implementation of functional testing processes including aging testing and optical performance (Lead: LEITAT,
Partners: SAL, CSEM, VTT, TNO, Fraunhofer, IRIS) In this task functional testing, WVTR and ageing testing processes

determine aging effect and shelf life and validation study with synthetic analytes (following ISO 23640:2011) (LEITAT).
Performance related to generation (solar cells) and luminescence (OLED) will be tested, before, during, and after each
test (LEITAT).
LEITAT, VTT, ASPF will test solar cells, OLEDs and whole system in natural conditions for outdoor applications and
indoor applications. All the results of test will feed to WP2 in order to minimize defects and improve whole performance.
Further;

• Roll-to-Roll and Sheet-to-Sheet inspection on OLED and (O)PV devices to detect defects responsible for aging and

VTT will develop a R2R setup to measure IV characteristics of OLED and solar cell rolls. The setup will contain also

CSEM: will perform other tests e.g. mechanical stress testing and Characterization of the optical performance by a)

T4.1 Identification of production automation requirements and process control system design (Lead: ASPF, Partner:
Solibro, BUL, Smit TS, TWI,DTF, IRIS): In this task, identification of production automation requirements will be done
for S2S and roll to roll manufacturing processes. These will inform the design of the process control system that will

T4.2 Sensor system development for automation and monitoring of production processes (Lead: IRIS, Partner: VTT,
DTF, Coatema, Solibro, ASPF, TWI)
Subtask 4.2.1 Selection of suite of sensors and adaptation to process requirements
A suite of commercial sensors will be selected to follow the production process and automation parameters defined in
T4.1 (e.g. temperature, pressure, material use rate, color, viscosity, power/energy consumption). It is envisaged that these
sensors will detect and relay information about key strategic production-specific attributes in real-time and online or
inline (depending on the process stage). The parameters measured will be carefully selected so as to correlate with each
other and with the optical inspection systems developed under WP3 and enable an online and real-time quality control of
production. In as much as possible the equipment used will integrate the following features to facilitate automation and
the possibility of closed loop control: MicroElectroMechanical systems (MEMS)-based components; “smart” sensors
that have intelligent readout electronics and the microsensor in a single chip; zero power ‘flow tracker’ sensors. Sensors
used in this development will be acquired from commercial sources and will be strategically conditioned and tailored for
providing affordable monitoring capability and enhanced product monitoring additional to the optical and NDT systems
developed in the previous WPs.
SubTask 4.2.2 System integration and performance evaluation: A key performance marker of these sensors is their
ability to be delivered as a single integrated system, seamlessly embedded in each of the pilot lines. This work will
include the integration of the sensors on a common, single, multifunctional board that meets the requirement that each
single sensor will have a standardized output in the desired outputs in the proper format for the use in the intelligent
decision management and control software developed in T4.3. In addition, this board will provide the capability of both
wired and wireless data transfer for maximizing flexibility and ensuring optimal integration with the process. In the
context of this sub-task, IRIS will deliver one such board for each pilot and/or production line as necessary.
T4.3 Development of intelligent decision management and control software for automation and monitoring (Lead: IRIS,
Partner: BUL, ASPF): In this task intelligent decision management software will be designed integrating inputs from
manufacturing processes, inline inspection systems and production line sensors. This quality and control platform should
be a common system for all pilot lines where sensors will be attached, and data will be acquired in order to apply, if
needed, model treatment for statistical process control. In case a common system is not possible, minor tweaks will be
applied to the platform for specific pilot lines to allow the sensors in that process to be integrated within the platform
The platform will be composed of several layers such as the data acquisition layer, the data treatment and storage layer,
data mining layer (designed to facilitate the machine learning algorithms developed under T3.8), and the User Interface.
The output data will be used for process optimization and control with a process control layer added in case closed
loop control is employed.
Work by ASPF relates to i) Monitoring of environment and processes, ii) Scheduling of tasks, machines, and employees,
iii) Report generation for runs and machines, iv) Product data connected to machine, v) Machine parameter central
collection, and vi) Inventory management and assistants, in order to establish a Smart Factory.

Work package number 9 WP5 Lead beneficiary 10 11 - LEITAT
Work package title Development of processes for materials recycling, repair and re-use approaches for
OLEDs and solar cells
Start month 13 End month 36
Objectives
From a life cycle perspective, sustainability of OLEDs and solar cell products as well as their respective manufacturing
process must be ensured from different approaches such as:
(1) the optimal selection and use of raw materials for manufacturing,
(2) avoiding and/or minimising waste generation along the production line, including the small production of discarded
materials by the quality control system, and
(3) guaranteeing in the recyclability of these products and by-products during manufacturing from:
(3a) an easy dismantling/ separation of target materials for reuse into the own production line, and
(3b) to ensure their compatibility with potential existing pre-treatment technologies and recycling solutions for complete
valorisation when possible (or valorisation of components defined and agreed with end-users) and reuse into the
production line.
The WP5 has as a double objective (1) to ensure the recyclability of the end-of-life products and/or by-products generated
by OLEDSOLAR manufacturing processes, and (2) the feasibility evaluation of existing and/or innovative recycling
routes for the reuse of discarded materials as new raw materials into the own manufacturing process. Proposed recycling
technologies will be tested and validated at laboratory scale and finally selected for scaled-up at a representative pilot
scale according to the previous lab results.
Description of work and role of partners
WP5 - Development of processes for materials recycling, repair and re-use approaches for OLEDs and solar
cells [Months: 13-36]
LEITAT, ASPF, CSEM, Solibro, DTF, Advent, INURU, SMIT
Description of work
This goal will be achieved by the development of the following tasks:
T5.1 Evaluation of potential existing pre-treatment technologies and recycling solutions for OLEDs and solar cells
(M13-M26). (Lead: LEITAT, Partners: CSEM, ADVENT): Selected end-of-life products from OLEDSOLAR end-users
and materials suppliers, and/or those discarded after inspection and quality control system (WP3) will be evaluated in
terms of potential recyclability. For that, a first task focused on the identification of wastes along the manufacturing
process will be performed. Identification of their generation and their recycling/reuse potential will be performed
by using Key Performance Indicators (KPI). From selected wastes/materials, physic-chemical characteristics will be
evaluated as well as their cost/economic potential. From results, procedures for correct handling and separation of
wastes by categories defining potential reuse/recycling will be designed. Then, in order to guarantee the recyclability
of separated wastes, the following recycling approaches will be proposed and evaluated:
- Manual sorting and selection of added value materials (pre-concentration) before external recycling (existing
technologies),
- Grinding/homogenisation of wastes for external recycling (existing technologies)
Regarding the first two options, analysis of existing pre-treatment technologies and conventional recycling solutions
will be performed. The analysis will include cost-competitive and mature solutions (TRL9) as well as novel technologies
(TRL6-8). Both for mature and novel technologies, feasibility tests at laboratory and pilot scale (according to previous
lab results) will be performed in Task 5.3. External treatment and recycling cost will be compared with the cost of the
novel raw material used in OLEDSOLAR production processes.
T5.2 Definition of existing and/or innovative recycling routes into the own manufacturing process (M18-M36) (Lead:
LEITAT, Partners: CSEM, Smit TS): According to the physic-chemical characteristics of those wastes already identified
and quantified in Task 5.1, potentially generated from the scalable production line of OLEDSOLAR products (OLEDs
and solar thin films), existing and/or innovative recycling routes into the own manufacturing process will be analysed.
For that, manufacturing requirements will be checked, and potential reuse of wastes will be evaluated. Quality criteria
such as potential loose of material properties (i.e. reuse of green solvents, semiconductors, reuse of anodes discarded
by the quality control system, etc.) will be considered.
T5.3 Testing and validating the feasibility of recycling technologies at laboratory/pilot scale (M20-M36) (Lead: LEITAT,
Partners: ASPF, CSEM, Smit TS, INURU): Mature and novel pre-treatment and recycling technologies proposed in
Page 26 of 45
Task 5.1 will be firstly tested and evaluated at laboratory scale. These solutions may include mechanical (i.e. cutting,
grinding), thermal (i.e. heating) and/or chemical (i.e. leaching) technologies. Feasibility of proposed solutions will be
evaluated from techno-economic and environmental points of view in WP6 (T6.5) and quantified by using the KPIs
defined in Task 5.1. Testing of recycled materials will be performed by LEITAT and CSEM, in order to evaluate
the quality of the recycled material. Recovered materials may be semiconductors (e.g. ITO), electrodes (e.g. silver),
substrates (e.g. PET), among others. Performance of selected recycled materials will be evaluated against reference
material. Scale-up tests at a representative pilot scale will be performed according to the previous lab results.

T6.5 Quantification of the environmental and economic performance of the OLEDSOLAR solution (Lead: LEITAT,
Partners: All): In this task quantification of environmental and economic performance will be done including Life Cycle
Assessment and a Life Cycle Costing. A combination of the LCA and LCC is important to analyse the relation between
Page 29 of 45
environmental and cost performance in order to prove the sustainability of the manufacturing process and the final
products (OLED and organic solar cell).A comprehensive LCA based on international standards ISO 14040 and updated
publications of the European Platform on Life Cycle Assessment and the European Commission (ILCD Handbook, PEF
Guidelines) will be performed to quantify and analyse the potential environmental impacts of the products.The LCC,
according to SETAC Guidelines and other existing initiatives, will evaluate the total costs of the system throughout its
entire life cycle, considering all costs arising from raw materials to production, use phase and end-of-life. The LCA and
LCC will allow characterising the sustainability performance of the OLEDSOLAR products generated in OLEDSOLAR
project. The involvement of the rest of the partners is a key issue since they will provide the data obtained in previous
tasks and WP.
T6.6 Standardisation, regulations, certification and safety codes (Lead: TWI, Partner: BUL): A standardization
methodology (protocols) of opto-electronic component and packaged devices will be developed. To achieve a high-
volume production, reliability testing and appropriate standards for qualification of optical devices e.g. OLEDs, CIGS
and OPVs should be established. Those standards are mandatory as it serves the industry as a baseline for implementation
of pilot-line production. However, in many cases reliability is not even studied or confirmed specially in R2R scale
up production. As a result, the missing prove of long-term reliability of devices reduces their level of acceptance
considerably. The standard protocols will be based on MIL standards already applied in microelectronics domain. The
standard will cover the functionality of the components and will develop and apply accelerated aging tests to guarantee
functionality of components for at least 10 years. Statistical analysis will be considered for representative testing during
mass production. Ageing at accelerated conditions like high temperature, thermal cycling, cooling and/or heating in
controlled cooling rate will be applied. Pass/fail criteria will be based on evaluation of the devices performance with
respect to defined tolerances.
