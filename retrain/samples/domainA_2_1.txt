=== Domain A: NIR spectroscopy for real-time food quality control in smart manufacturing lines ===
=== Section 2_1: Expected outcomes and impacts ===

4.1 Expected impacts

The proposed project aims to develop a new NIR spectroscopy system in the context of the I4MS initiative for the monitoring of food quality in smart manufacturing lines. The system will be able to monitor the quality and safety parameters of food products during the different processing steps and to provide information to the operators in real time by means of a user-friendly interface. The expected impacts of the new NIR system are listed below and organized according to the impact categories described in the call text:

4.1.1 Economic impacts

The NIR system proposed in this project will be able to control the quality of food products during the processing steps in real time, allowing the food manufacturers to react immediately to any variation in the process and to take corrective actions. This will allow reducing the variability of the final product and to enhance the product quality and safety. The reduction of the variability will also allow to reduce the amount of returned products. The possibility of obtaining information about the quality of the products during the processing steps will allow to reduce the number of samples that have to be taken and analysed in the laboratory, reducing the sampling errors and the time lost in the analysis of the samples. The automation of the quality control and the possibility of monitoring the quality and safety parameters of the products during the processing steps will also reduce the costs of the quality control for the food manufacturers. The possibility of taking immediate corrective actions will reduce the amount of rejected product, with a positive impact on the costs of the food manufacturers. The quality control systems currently used in the food industry are often based on the analysis of samples, which have to be taken from the production line and then transported to the laboratory for analysis. The quality control of the products during the different processing steps is usually not performed, with the consequence that, in case of incorrect process parameters or in case of a contamination of the product, the problem is detected only once the products are finalized. This can lead to the rejection of the final product, with consequent economic losses for the food manufacturers. The possibility of having an effective quality and safety control of the products during the processing steps will allow to reduce the variability of the final product and to avoid the rejection of entire batches of product, with consequently a higher efficiency of the production process. The possibility of having a real-time quality and safety control will also allow to improve the quality of the products, enhancing the competitiveness of the European food industry. The NIR system will be able to provide information about many parameters of the products, giving the possibility to the food manufacturers to realize new product characteristics, to increase the number of parameters to be controlled and to reduce the variability of the product. The automation of the quality control will also allow a more efficient use of the human resources of the food manufacturers, as the operators will be able to focus their work on other activities. The NIR system will be able to monitor the quality and safety parameters of food products in real time during the processing steps and to provide information to the operators in real time. The information will be accessible by means of a user-friendly interface, which will be developed according to the requirements of the users. The system will be able to communicate the information to the operators by means of alarms and messages, warning the operators in case of the occurrence of problems and suggesting possible corrective actions to be taken. The possibility of having an immediate response from the quality control system will allow to reduce the reaction times and to improve the final quality of the products. The possibility of having an immediate response will reduce the need of the presence of the operators, making the quality control system more flexible and allowing to monitor more production lines. The use of NIR spectroscopy for the quality control of food products is currently limited to the laboratory analysis of samples, but the use of NIR spectroscopy in real-time quality control is very promising. The development of the NIR system proposed in the project will have a positive impact on the EU food industry, by increasing the competitiveness of the European food manufacturers and by creating new jobs and new business opportunities.

4.1.2 Social impacts

The main social impact of the proposed project will be the development of a system that will allow improving the quality and safety of food products. This will be carried out by using a non-invasive sensor, which will allow the operators to monitor the quality and safety parameters of food products during the processing steps and to take corrective actions in case of process deviations and product contaminations. The possibility of having a real-time quality and safety control will allow to reduce the number of foodborne illnesses, thus improving the health and welfare of European citizens. The possibility of having a real-time quality and safety control will also increase the consumers’ confidence in the safety of food products and in the food quality control systems, improving the acceptance of the new technologies.
