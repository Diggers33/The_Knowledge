3.2 Capacity of participants and consortium as a whole #@CON-SOR-CS@# #@PRJ-MGT-PM@# The SecureFood proposal has been developed through close the collaboration between its multi-disciplinary, multi-sectorial, multi-national consortium. Consortium members were selected to ensure complementary and excellent coverage of all required disciplinary expertise, sectorial position, engineering, and scientific excellence, as well as relevant financial and administrative capacities for successful commercial exploitation of project results. The Consortium consists of 35 partners from 18 EU and Associated Countries. The partners are regionally distributed and provide extensive economic and cultural diversity. Thus, the broadest possible set of requirements will be ensured, along with testing and validation activities against different settings, which will contribute to the universal applicability of the approach. The Consortium combines different critical players on the various aspects one needs to address for a comprehensive approach to safeguard food security and enhance food systems resilience. Being a user-centric project that follows a multi-actor approach, the project has engaged many food systems stakeholders (end-users) alongside the food supply chain, namely UAC and UCAB representing agriculture producers and agriculture companies in Ukraine, ROUS being a dairy producer and supplier in Greece, BIGH being an aquaculture producer in Belgium, SPES and its 9 affiliated entities (FEDAL, ANIA, FIPA, FIAB, SEVT, SETBİR, CCIS, LVA and FFDI) being national food and drink federations (industrial actors) in Italy, France, Portugal, Spain, Greece, Turkey, Slovenia, Austria and Check Republic, MC and MCH being the leading food retailers in Portugal, EKP (NGO) being the association of consumers in Greece, COSMO being a Cyprus bulk carrier and ELAF, being a cold-chain road transport carrier in Greece. Moreover, the Ukrainian Ministry of Agrarian Policy and Food (MINAG) is a consortium partner, being a governmental entity responsible, among others, for the Ukrainian agricultural policy and food security. SecureFood also includes two organizations that collaborate with national Ministries, i.e.  LUKE that is a research organization operating under the Ministry of Agriculture and Forestry of Finland and will provide all data needed for the dairy products value chain, as well as ELGO that is a legal entity of the public sector under the auspices of the Ministry of Agricultural Development and Food in Greece and will provide all relevant information for the milk and fish value chains in Greece. In addition, SecureFood provides the involvement of leading academic organizations and research institutes, including IAMO institute in Germany that specializes in economic, social and political processes of change in the agricultural and food sector and, in the project, will develop digital solutions (3D XR-based simulator) and the agent-based Agricultural Policy Simulator (AgriPoliS), ZLC that is an institute established by the Government of Aragon in Spain in partnership with the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and the University of Zaragoza that will provide the modelling for digital twin development and will lead the foresight analysis and exploratory modelling, Laurea University of Applied Sciences in Finland (LAUR) that will bring its expertise for socioeconomic and ethical management aspects as well as know-how on living labs, ICCS institute that will take over the technical management and will also develop the resilient route planning tool, and the National University of Life and Environmental Sciences in Ukraine (NULES) that will provide the economic modelling and will also support the Ukrainian case study. The consortium also involves DNV, that brings its huge expertise on risk analysis and management, EMP (SME), which will develop the resilience management tool and will manage the organization and implementation of cases studies, GL (SME), which has a huge record of research activities on food loss and food waste and will develop the food loos and waste modelling and tool, and CARR (SME) that will lead the dissemination and communication activities. In addition, the consortium involves four IT-related companies: ED (project coordinator) that has proven expertise in the management and integration of large R&D projects and will take over the development of the early warning system, INNOV (SME) that will develop the information exchange platform and take over the standardization activities, IRIS (SME) that is responsible for the supply chain digital twin development and EXUS (SME) that will develop the observatory dashboard.
