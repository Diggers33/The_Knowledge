For external characterization services provided by laboratories equipped with advanced 
and more suitable instrumentation for the evaluation of coating properties. 
This reallocation reflects the strategic shift we adopted during the project implementation, 
prioritizing access to high-performance external facilities over in-house equipment 
acquisition. These services have been crucial for the technical development and validation 
of our coatings, allowing for more accurate, efficient, and reliable characterization. In 
particular, a significant portion of the external services are based on X-ray Fluorescence 
(XRF) analysis, which has proven essential to support our work on PVD coatings with 
complex multilayer structures and variable compositions, including the incorporation of 
silver at different concentrations. The XRF technique provides several key advantages: 
•Precise elemental composition analysis: It enables the identification and quantification of 
elements in the coatings, ensuring that the intended material composition—including silver 
content—is correctly achieved. 
•Verification of layer uniformity: XRF allows us to check the homogeneity of the coatings 
across the surface, which is critical for process validation and quality assurance. 
•Multilayer structure insight: Although not a direct thickness measurement technique, XRF 
helps in detecting variations in the elemental distribution across multilayer structures, 
supporting the fine-tuning of our deposition processes. 
•Non-destructive and rapid: The non-destructive nature of XRF, combined with fast 
turnaround and low cost, makes it an ideal tool for regular analysis during the optimization 
and prototype production phases. Therefore, the use of XRF-equipped external labs has 
been strategically important to meet the project’s technical objectives and ensure high-
quality, reproducible coatings. 
To justify these costs, a service contract covering the execution of the external tests during 
the project period is fundamental. This contract outlines the scope of work, including the 
number and type of analyses to be performed, and can be used to classify the related 
expenses as “services” rather than subcontracting, in line with the criteria you outlined.
