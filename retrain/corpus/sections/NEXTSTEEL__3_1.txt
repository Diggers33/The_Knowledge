3.1 Work plan and resources

Brief presentation of the overall structure of the work plan

NextSteel work plan is structured to deliver a cohesive, cross-disciplinary response to the challenges of carbon-neutral steelmaking. From the 5 research and innovation blocks that underpin the methodology, the work plan has been designed around 8 Work Packages (WPs).
It follows a clear technology-maturation and system-integration flow (as presented in the methodology):

Block 1: Bio-anthracite and biogenic hydrogen-rich from biowaste for Low-CO2 steelmaking: WP1 focuses on bioanthracite production from biowastes, optimising conversion routes (HTT, leaching, pyrolysis) and scaling them from TRL 4 to TRL 6. The WP ensures that bioanthracite meets the stringent quality and safety requirements for steelmaking. WP2 targets the recovery of green hydrogen from aqueous phases via SCWG/AD, closing the loop on biowaste valorisation while maximising carbon recovery rates. Validation tests at lab and demo scale will be performed in WP3
Block 2: Circularity by reusing steel waste for new green steel processes WP3 also integrates circular outputs with steelmaking residues (e.g. slag, metal fines) to create usable reductants and feedstocks, validating these combinations in three production routes: BF–BOF, EAF, and DRI. Additionally, the use of green gas as an alternative to natural gas for use in reheating furnaces will be also studied.
Block 3: Energy integration and efficiency: WP4 addresses energy integration across steelmaking and biorefinery units, exploring reuse of EAF waste heat to power bioconversion units, use of pyrogas for electricity generation, and improving the energy balance of the entire process chain.
Block 4: Digitalisation and process intelligence: WP5 develops HoloSteel, a digital intelligence layer integrating Process Analytical Technologies (PAT), Quality by Design (QbD), real-time monitoring, and predictive simulation. This ensures stable operation and decision support under variable input conditions. WP5 also ensures data interoperability with the DIGITAL-61 initiative and provides digital feedback to upstream and downstream WPs.
Block 5: Industry 5.0, business case, and ecosystem engagement: WP6 anchors the human and organisational transformation, co-developing operational roles, training content, ESG strategies, and business models with industrial stakeholders. WP7 leads outreach, standardisation, clustering, and impact valorisation, maximising alignment with EU missions (e.g. Clean Steel Partnership, Net-Zero Industry Act) and ensuring that key exploitable results feed into industrial deployment and policy.
WP4 cuts across all blocks (and particularly Block 3), providing a comprehensive impact assessment (LCA, LCC, social impact, risk analysis), benchmarking NextSteel’s value chain against conventional steelmaking processes and supporting informed scale-up.
WP8 ensures coordination, consortium management, and alignment with cross-project EU frameworks.
This plan is built to maximise synergy across disciplines (materials, digital, energy, and social sciences) and ensure tight cross-WP integration via shared milestones and data feedback loops, e.g. pilot data from WP3 feeds into both the HoloSteel refinement in WP5 & training design in WP6, while the impact analysis in WP4 informs business strategy (WP6) and policy dialogue (WP7).
