Objectives 
The objectives of WP2 are to develop a best practice approach to assessing the impact of SPIRE and similar projects 
which will in turn be integrated with a proactive set of tools and guidance aimed at enhancing project impact applicable 
to each of the six scales of potential stakeholder interest set out in Section 1.1. Specifically, WP2 will:  
i) Develop a framework for impact assessment appropriate to projects of the type undertaken in SPIRE.  
ii) Seek to understand current barriers to full implementation of project outputs in industry. 
iii) Develop guidance and tools to facilitate effective promotion of project outputs to industrial and other 
stakeholdersiv) Identify and develop educational resources to facilitate knowledge transfer and training about outputs 
from SPIRE and similar projects. 
v) Integrate the above into a draft methodology for impact assessment and enhancement.  
Description of work and role of partners

LEEDS, BRITEST, INEOS, ASPIRE, IRIS, CRIT 
Task 2.A1: Review metrics for measuring project progress, impact and success from FoF-Impact, SPIRE 4, industry & 
projects within SPRING (BRITEST, All, M1-2) 
BRITEST will lead the work to collate progress metrics and methods from consortium projects to hel p assess which 
should be tested on the SPRING case study projects (e.g. proposals from STYLE regarding use of qualitative 
sustainability metrics at review points). 
Task 2.A2: Research other good practice for project progress/ impact/ success metrics (LEEDS, with input from CRIT, 
M1-3) 
LEEDS will lead the work to review good practice in evidencing the progress of research and innovation projects 
towards impact. It will assess existing approaches in order to synthesise a framework and a set of metrics appropr iate 
to SPIRE projects but embedded within a broad understanding of the innovation process. The framework will support 
assessment of achieved impact and identification of opportunities for further dissemination and impact-oriented actions. 
In this latter mode, it would act as a diagnostic tool and prescriptive device, potentially capable of being applied more 
than once during a project’s development path. 
Task 2.B1: Review high -level project outputs from case studies and select SPIRE projects (BRITEST, All,  M1-3) 
BRITEST will lead this task to catalogue high level outputs from the case study projects, to enable a structure to be

3) LEEDS will work with BRITEST to review approaches to structuring information from projects for educational

Task 2.C1: Review available technology scanning approaches (CRIT, with input from LEEDS, M3 -6) 
CRIT will work with LEEDS to review approaches to technology scanning. These are techniques used to ensure that 
projects are aware of developments in innovations and markets relevant to their subject area, so that projects are not 
being run in isolation of external developments. Examples include social media threads used in EPOS and commercial 
tools used by CRIT. 
Task 2.D1: Review best practice for enabling good decision making when evaluating project outputs (LEEDS, with

LEEDS will lead this task to support well -founded decisions about possible uptake of project outputs, tai lored to each

from stakeholders external to the project and supply -side advice about ways for projects to move forward with 
promoting their outputs in ways appropriate to the output and to the scale and level of external interest. Effective 
communication of messages to external stakeholders to facilitate their decision making will form an important element 
of this support process. 
Task 2.D2: Review current barriers to full implementation in industry (BRITEST, with input from industry partners 
and stakeholders, M5-8) 
BRITEST will lead work to look at specific case studies to document the barriers to widespread industrial uptake. The 
aim is to improve understanding of hurdles that successful projects have had to cross, and reasons why some projects 
have had more limited industrial uptake.
