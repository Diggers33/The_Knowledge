4. Members of the Consortium 
4.1. Member 1: IRIS Technology Solutions S.A. 
We are IRIS Technology Solutions S.A., a SME based in Athens, Greece. Our company was founded by a group of scientists in 2004, with the aim to seek, develop and provide innovative solutions based on novel analytical techniques to various industrial sectors. Our main focus is on Near Infrared Spectroscopy, a technique that can be used for rapid and non-destructive determination of a variety of materials’ chemical composition and physical properties, and is widely used in agriculture and food industries. 
We provide integrated solutions based on Near Infrared Spectroscopy, including hardware, software and services, to our customers, mainly companies active in agriculture, food, pharmaceutical and chemical industries. Our main objective is to help our customers increase their productivity and efficiency, through providing real-time analysis information that can be used in real-time process control. 
We have extensive experience in Near Infrared Spectroscopy and its applications in various industrial sectors. We have participated in 8 research projects (5 as coordinators) funded by European Programs in the past 15 years and have a vast network of contacts in our industry, which we use to introduce and disseminate our products and services. 
Our company holds a number of patents and has published more than 30 papers in international scientific journals and conferences. 
4.2. Member 2: (University of Bologna) 
We are the University of Bologna (UNIBO), a leading institution in higher education and research, with a long history dating back to 1088. Our university is organized in 33 Departments and 11 Schools, and has a student population of more than 87,000. 
Our research activities are carried out in over 500 laboratories, with the University of Bologna having the highest number of patents among Italian universities. 
The Department of Agricultural and Food Sciences (DISTAL) was established in 2012 and is dedicated to a broad range of scientific and technological fields, including agriculture, forestry, food, animal husbandry, veterinary science, and industrial and environmental sciences. 
The Food Process Engineering Group in the Department of Agricultural and Food Sciences conducts research and education in the field of food process engineering. The group is composed of 12 faculty members, along with post-doctoral researchers, research fellows, and PhD students. 
The Food Process Engineering Group is actively involved in research projects, both nationally and internationally, funded by public bodies and the industry. Our research activities focus on the development of innovative technologies for food processing, with a particular emphasis on process intensification and sustainability. 
We are committed to innovation in the agri-food sector and conduct research in the field of process engineering for the transformation of food and agricultural products. Our research interests include thermal and non-thermal processing technologies, process intensification, food preservation, novel food packaging, and food process modelling and optimization. 
The Food Process Engineering Group has a strong publication record, with over 500 papers published in international journals, books, book chapters, and proceedings. Our research outputs contribute to the advancement of food process engineering knowledge and its application in the agri-food industry. 
4.3. Member 3: (CNR - Institute of Marine Sciences) 
We are the National Research Council (CNR) of Italy, a public research organization that carries out multidisciplinary and high-quality research activities in various sectors, including food. Established in 1923, CNR is the largest public research organization in Italy and is subject to the direction and coordination of the Ministry of Education, Universities, and Research. 
The Institute of Marine Sciences (ISMAR) is one of the Institutes that make up CNR and is dedicated to marine science research. It is distributed over several sites across Italy and covers a wide range of scientific disciplines related to marine sciences. 
The Department of Chemical, Materials and Industrial Production Engineering (DCIMP) at the University of Naples Federico II conducts research and education in chemical, materials, and industrial production engineering. It is composed of 21 faculty members and over 100 affiliated research staff, including post-doctoral researchers, research fellows, and PhD students. 
The Department has laboratories dedicated to chemical and food process engineering and has laboratories equipped for research and education in the field of chemical, materials, and industrial production engineering. 
We are actively involved in research projects funded by public bodies and the industry, both nationally and internationally. Our research activities cover a wide range of topics related to chemical, materials, and industrial production engineering, with a focus on sustainable and innovative processes. 
The Department of Chemical, Materials and Industrial Production Engineering is committed to contributing to the advancement of knowledge and innovation in the field of chemical, materials, and industrial production engineering. We have a strong publication record, with over 100 scientific papers published in international journals, books, book chapters, and proceedings.
