3.1.1 INTRODUCTION TO THE WORK PLAN   
IS2H4C will be implemented over 48 months as an appropriate timeframe based on a consortium that 
is experienced with IS implementations. The work plan is structured into 8 Work Packages (WPs). The 
project management and governance activities will drive the entire work plan (WP1), which will be cross -
cut with impact -oriented dissemination and exploitation activities (WP8). The scientific and technical 
activities will be undertaken in an interrela ted workflow spanning WP2 to 7. As described in section 1.2, 
the different WPs (2, 3, 4, 5) represent interconnected compartments of work which are eventually 
integrated into the H4C demonstration (WP6) and the digital collaboration platform built in WP7. The 
technical management of the project (WP1), undertaken by UT, will ensure the full coherence and 
alignment of the work comprised by each WP towards fulfilling the project objectives. WP2, led by AEE, 
will identify and evaluate the circularity pathways within each H4C (German, Dutch, Basque and Turkish) 
to further develop and integrate advanced technologies. To include the participation of the surrounding 
ecosystems into the hubs, WP3, led by TUDO, will analyse the local setting, identify relevant stakeholders 
to engage with and collect useful insight for the improvement of the hub. WP4, led by UT, will utilise AI, 
operation research and agent-based simulation to ensure efficient operations and expansion of each H4C 
and overcome obstacles along the value chain. WP5, led by KPMG, will conduct a comprehensive analysis 
of existing business models in CE and IS, identify their strengths and weaknesses, and develop a new 
business model that capitalizes on emerging industry trends and technologies. Integrating the research 
and knowledg e gathered from previous WPs, WP6, led by PROV, will test and demonstrate the 
technologies developed in WP2 in each H4C. The technologies’ performance will be evaluated to identify 
possible optimisation areas. Finally, WP7, led by IRIS, will build a  userfriendly, scalable and adaptable 
Digital Collaboration Platform for Circularity Hubs (Digital H4C) which will facilitate the sharing or 
resources and data among participating entities, fostering collaboration and transparency.

3.2  CAPACITY OF PARTICIPANTS AND CONSORTIUM AS A WHOLE  
IS2H4C consortium brings together 32 partners from 10 member states (Netherlands, Spain, Portugal, 
Belgium, Turkey, Austria, Germany, Greece, Ireland and Belgium) and 10 members from 8 countries for 
the International Advisory Board (France, Austria, Sweden, Italy , Netherlands, Denmark, Canada, USA) 
which have been selected for their outstanding R&D, innovation performance and track record. Industry, 
financial, logistics, technological and academic partners lead the way in their respective areas of action 
across the Industrial Symbiosis network. Each member will have a distinctive role and will ensure that the 
results will be exploited in an efficient way. IS2H4C involves a multidisciplinary group comprising four types 
of entities: (i) Research centres/universities (33% of the consortium, accounting for 44% of the budget), (ii) 
large industries (24% of the consortium, 20%  of the budget), (iii) SMEs (22% of the consortium, 19% of 
the budget), (iv) NGOs (21% of the consortium, 17% of the budget) which operate at differ ent stages of 
the project. UT, IS2H4C’s project coordinator, has participated in several circular economy projects funded 
by different entities such as: (i) Designing sustainable second generation bioenergy supply chains: an 
agentbased modelling approach (Tech4People call); (ii) Secure Management Platform for Shared Process 
Resources (SHAREBOXH2020); (iii) Engineering an Information Platform for Sustainable, Smart, and 
Resilient Circular Construction Ecosystems (BMS Signature proposal), and more recently (i v) Serious 
gaming for Circular Economy Transition (SG4CET, WSV innovative teaching proposals). UT is #1 at Times 
Higher Education ranking for SDG 9 “Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure”. UT is #6 in the world and #1 
in Europe by a total of 31 Scopus-indexed articles in the field of “Industrial Symbiosis”, being Devrim 
Yazan26 (project coordinator) ranked #2 author in the world by number of articles in this field (author #1, 
Luca Fraccascia, is member of the advisory board). Devrim is an associate professor at the Industrial 
Engineering and Business Information Systems (IEBIS) Department.

General  
During the execution of the IS2H4C project appropriate occupational health and safety will be followed by 
all researchers. The beneficiaries will ensure that all ethics issues related to activities in the grant are 
addressed in compliance with ethical principles, the applicable internationa l and national law, and the 
provisions set out in the Grant Agreement. This includes the ethics issues identified in this report and any 
additional ethics issues that may emerge in the course of the grant. In case any substantial new ethics 
issues arise, b eneficiaries should inform the granting authority. In the following sections the IS2H4C 
consortium's ethical approach towards, personal data, non-EU countries, Environmental health and safety, 
Artificial Intelligence as well as Recommendations and adoption of the Ethics Summary Report outcomes 
will be outlined.
4.1 Personal data  
IS2H4C will adhere to legislative requirements of the General Data Protection Regulation. In case any 
activity of project requires the collection and processing of personal data during the project lifetime, the 
beneficiaries collecting, processing and hosting the  data will fulfil the specific laws (i.e. General Data 
Protection Regulation, GDPR, EU2016/679) This will also be safeguarded in close cooperation with the 
Data Protection Officer (DPO). The beneficiaries confirm that they are aware of the following  rules for 
collection and processing of personal data:  
• The host institution must confirm that it has appointed a DPO and the contact details of the DPO 
are made available to all data subjects involved in the research. For host institutions not required 
to appoint a DPO under the GDPR a detailed data protection policy for the project must be kept on 
file.  
• A description of the technical and organisational measures that will be implemented to safeguard 
the rights and freedoms of the data subjects/research participants must be submitted as a 
deliverable.  
Detailed information on the informed consent procedures in regard to data processing must be kept on 
file.
4.2 Non-EU countries   
In case any activity of project requires to be performed in a non -EU country, also these activities will be 
performed according to highest ethical standards, and applicable international, EU and national law, also 
taking all relevant ethical aspects into account.
4.3 Environmental health and safety   
IS2H4C does not raise considerable risks to health and safety of researchers and participant and does not 
involve the use of potentially harmful substances, processes, technologies, organisms or materials in a 
manner that raises significant concerns about individual security or safety or the protection of public health. 
UT (Coordinator) complies with National and European legislation on Protection of Environment and 
Occupational Safety. All IS2H4C’ partners have their own Occupational Health and safety procedures that 
comply with their national legislation.

4.5 Recommendations and adoption of the Ethics Summary Report outcomes  
The work package WP9, dedicated to Ethics has been added to the work plan of the project. The IS2H4C 
consortium will appoint an independent external Ethics Advisor (EA) who has proven competences and 
expertise, providing advice on the ethical aspects appropriate to the complexity of the ethical aspects, in 
the project context at project start. The mandat e of the EA would e.g. include an advisory role and 
assessments concerning the ethical merits of the work performed and providing independent 
recommendations. The exact details of the mandate will be outlined in a Memorandum of Understanding. 
The recommendations of the Ethics Advisor will be followed up closely and all activities performed in 
relation to ethics will be summarised in a report.
