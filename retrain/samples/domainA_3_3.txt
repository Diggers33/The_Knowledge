=== Domain A: NIR spectroscopy for real-time food quality control in smart manufacturing lines ===
=== Section 3_3: Consortium as a whole ===

2 Excellence

2.1 Objectives

This project aims to develop and validate the technological and functional concept of a mass-market NIR (Near Infrared) device to be installed in smart manufacturing lines in order to perform real-time food quality control tailored to the food producers’ needs.

The objectives of the project are:

• O1. Develop the technological concept to integrate NIR spectroscopy in smart manufacturing lines for real-time food quality control.

• O2. Develop a prototype of a mass-market NIR device to be installed in smart manufacturing lines.

• O3. Develop an advanced calibration and data processing software tool (including AI tools) to provide a user-friendly interface and real-time information on the food quality.

• O4. Validate the device in real scenarios to demonstrate the benefits of the device.

• O5. Develop the IPR and exploitation strategy.

• O6. Disseminate project results and raise awareness about the new device.

• O7. Ensure an effective and efficient management of the project.

• O8. Develop a plan for the replication and sustainability of the project results.

• O9. Develop a business model and a plan to scale up the project results and enable their commercialization.

2.2 Relation to the Work Programme

The project is in line with the SFS-27-2020 call “Smart agri-food manufacturing lines for food quality control and safety”.

The project aims to develop the technological and functional concept of a mass-market NIR device to be installed in smart manufacturing lines in order to perform real-time food quality control tailored to the food producers’ needs.

The project has an excellent potential to address R&I activities in this topic since it will develop a demonstrator of a mass-market NIR device for food quality control in smart manufacturing lines. The project will leverage on the development of a technology that has enabled the development of a mass-market product (NIRONE sensor, developed by Spectral Engines) and will develop the concept of a new product with a disruptive technological and functional concept.

2.3 Concept and methodology

The project will be organized in 7 work packages (WP):

• WP1. Technological and functional concept for NIR spectrometer in smart manufacturing lines.

• WP2. Development of the mass-market NIR device.

• WP3. Development of the software tool.

• WP4. Validation of the mass-market NIR device and software tool.

• WP5. Exploitation and communication activities.

• WP6. Project management and coordination.

• WP7. Replication, sustainability, and scale up.

3 Impact

3.1 Expected impacts listed in the work programme

The project will significantly contribute to the impact established in the SFS-27-2020 call “Smart agri-food manufacturing lines for food quality control and safety”:

• Innovating food manufacturing lines and processes: the project will develop a technological concept of a mass-market NIR device to be installed in smart manufacturing lines in order to perform real-time food quality control tailored to the food producers’ needs.

• Improving food quality: by providing real-time information on the food quality, the project will enable the food producers to take key decisions in the food manufacturing process.

• Improving food safety: the project will enable the food producers to detect non-compliances and take actions to avoid contaminated food products.

• Improving food traceability and transparency: the project will enable the food producers to provide final customers with information about the food.

• Increasing the efficiency of the manufacturing processes: the project will reduce the human workload in the quality control of the food, enabling the reallocation of human resources to other relevant manufacturing tasks.

• Increasing the productivity of the manufacturing lines: the project will reduce the time to perform quality controls.

• Reducing the environmental impact of the manufacturing lines: by reducing the time to perform quality controls, the project will reduce the energy consumption of the manufacturing lines.

• Increasing the competitiveness of the European agri-food industry: the project will enable the European agri-food industry to offer food products with improved quality, safety, traceability, transparency, and lower environmental impact.
