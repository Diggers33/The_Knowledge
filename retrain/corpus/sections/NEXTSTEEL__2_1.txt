2.1 Project’s pathways towards impact

a) Unique contribution of project results to (1) the outcomes specified in this topic, and (2) the wider impacts, in the longer term, specified in the destinations in the work programme

(1) Contribution to the Expected Outcomes: NEXTSTEEL will make a tangible contribution to all five expected outcomes specified in the HORIZON-CL4-INDUSTRY-2025-01-TWIN-TRANSITION-37 call topic, as follows:
Validate innovative carbon-neutral iron and steel making solutions within a system-level approach and in consideration of diverse materials with varying quality (raw input materials & reductants mix) & energy needs.
NextSteel validates scalable, carbon-neutral iron and steelmaking solutions through a truly integrated systems-level approach, addressing the interdependencies between carbon neutral reductants, recycled materials, optimized energy use, digital intelligence, and human operations in real-world industrial contexts.  Steelmaking is a complex system, where variations in feedstock composition, reductant quality, and process conditions cascade through the production chain, influencing thermodynamics, kinetics, slag chemistry, emissions, and ultimately, product quality. The transition to low-CO₂ steelmaking further heightens this complexity by introducing more heterogeneous, bio-based and waste-derived inputs, such as bio-anthracite, process gas and green hydrogen, which challenge the stability and predictability of traditional processes. To address these heightened challenges, NextSteel integrates innovations across five interdependent system levels- feedstock mixing, process circularity, energy integration, digital intelligence and workforce capability- ensuring that the system as a whole remains stable, efficient, and capable of delivering high-quality steel under decarbonised operating conditions.
On the materials side, NextSteel converts diverse, underutilised into high-quality bioanthracite & green hydrogen gas, obtaining up to 70% biogenic carbon recovery. These alternative reductants are validated as substitutes for fossil-based coke, coal, and natural gas across all major steelmaking pathways (DRI–EAF, BF–BOF, EAF) and in SiMn alloy production, notably silicomanganese using C–SiO₂-rich bioanthracite & catalytic slag valorisation.
On the circular process side, NextSteel incorporates two novel slag reuse pathways: (1) using ferroalloy slag as a catalyst in methane cracking to produce hydrogen and carbon-rich residues for SiMn production, and (2) co-compacting EAF slag and metal powder with bioanthracite to produce high-density, stable reductant briquettes for injection in EAFs, reducing dust and enhancing foaming/carburising behaviour.
On the energy side, a comprehensive modelling activity integrates pilot data to identify waste heat reuse pathways, optimise integrated energy balances, and quantify the benefits of integrated biogenic routes. The modelling will also inform cost-performance comparisons between conventional and bio-based steelmaking systems.
On the digital level, HoloSteel, NextSteel’s modular digital intelligence layer, combines PATs, QbD, and a holonic multi-agent digital twin to deliver Industry4.0 grade real-time monitoring, predictive simulation, and adaptive decision support. This enables steel plants to maintain process stability, transparency, and flexibility, even under fluctuating feedstock quality and conditions.
On the human and organisational side, NextSteel co-develops new roles, training materials, and decision-support tools for operators and managers. These tools will be tested during the project and scaled post-project, ensuring workforce readiness for the digital and green transition in steel in alignment with Industry 5.0 principles.
By validating these innovations as a coherent, interoperable system, NextSteel will show that full-system decarbonisation of steelmaking is achievable without compromising quality, economic viability, or operability. Scaled to just 15% of EU steel plants by 2050, NextSteel could enable direct annual GHG reductions of up to 24 Mt CO₂, supporting the Clean Steel Partnership roadmap, Net-Zero Industry Act, and broader EU Green Deal objectives.
Address high-risk factors at macroscopic and microscopic level through detailed characterisation of the physical and chemical interactions that could compromise the optimal functioning of the processes
NextSteel systematically addresses high-risk factors that could compromise the reliable functioning of low-carbon steelmaking processes. This is done both at the macroscopic system level (e.g., process stability, route integration, energy efficiency) and at the microscopic material level (e.g., thermochemical interactions, slag chemistry, reduction kinetics, and surface behaviour). At the macroscopic level, the project will analyse the system-wide effects of introducing variable bio-based reductants (including bioanthracite, enriched hydrogen gas, and process gases) across the BF–BOF, EAF, and DRI routes. Particular attention will be given to understanding how these inputs influence process thermodynamics, slag formation, gas flow dynamics, heat transfer, and plant energy balances. This will be supported by integrated mass/energy modelling to quantify operational trade-offs & identify process sensitivities under scaled and integrated configurations. At the microscopic level, we will conduct in-depth multi-scale characterisation of key material interactions using advanced analytical tools (e.g., spectroscopy, microscopy, compositional analysis) in order to gain an understanding into how the heterogeneous physical and chemical properties of the novel bio-based reductants interact with molten metal, slag, and refractory linings. This will include, e.g. characterisation of bioanthracite compacted with EAF slag and metal powder, analysing its reactivity, pH, mechanical stability, and carburising behaviour; assessment of catalytic slag materials from SiMn/FeMn processes used in methane cracking, including their role in gas-phase hydrogen enrichment and carbon deposition; exploration of gas cracking pathways for process gas rich in CH₄ and CO, identifying risks of catalyst degradation, or side reactions. These empirical insights feed into the project’s Quality-by-Design (QbD) framework and are used to refine the predictive models and control logic embedded in HoloSteel—NextSteel’s holonic, agent-based digital intelligence layer. HoloSteel integrates real-time data from sensors and characterisation tools to enable proactive detection, simulation, and mitigation of deviations or anomalies that might otherwise compromise energy performance, material yield, or product quality. By linking micro-level material behaviour with macro-level process control and by embedding this intelligence into a closed-loop decision-support system, NextSteel will build a robust foundation for managing complexity and ensuring stable, high-quality, low-CO₂ steel production under real industrial conditions.
Solve system-level issues within at least two low-CO2 production routes
In NextSteel we address system-level challenges across three strategic low-CO₂ production routes: green H₂-based DRI + bioC-based EAF; EAF and BF with biogenic anthracite; and Silico-manganese production with C–SiO₂-rich bioanthracite. In each route, the project will tackle key system-level barriers (e.g. feedstock variability, energy efficiency, slag stability, process adaptability, and emissions control), by validating the controlled use of the biogenic reductants (bioanthracite and enriched hydrogen gas). Through a combination of pilot-scale validation (with our steelmaking partners AMMR, CELSA, ERA) and real-world operational data, we will ensure that: ■ Process performance remains stable despite the introduction of heterogeneous, low-cost feedstocks; ■ Energy balances are optimised via integration with site waste heat and gas flows; ■Slag and gas behaviours are controlled through co-compaction strategies, catalytic effects, and tailored chemistry; and, ■ Digital process control is enabled via the HoloSteel intelligence layer, whereby it uses PAT for real-time monitoring and its Holonics Agent-based DT for predictive simulation and QbD principles will support stable, adaptive, and low-emission operations across all three routes. Such a comprehensive systems-level approach will ensure that the new biogenic reductants are effectively embedded into the full production environment, with adjustments to upstream preparation, downstream injection, digital control, and plant integration strategies. This will result is a series of validated decarbonised steelmaking pathways that are technically feasible, industrially scalable, and digitally enabled, and ready for further deployment.
Define solutions and provide concepts to address possible modifications or material substitutions in innovative installations for low CO2 iron and steel production
The biorefinery pathways developed by NextSteel enable the substitution of fossil-based reductants and fluxes with renewable, biogenic alternatives derived from biowastes. NextSteel defines and validates solutions for integrating bioanthracite and green hydrogen into existing BF–BOF, EAF, and DRI steelmaking routes by proposing tailored material pretreatment strategies, such as fast HTC and chemical leaching, that aim to ensure consistent quality and compatibility with metallurgical requirements. These material substitutions will be supported by specific process modifications, such as co-compaction with EAF slag, catalytic cracking using Mn-rich residues, and adjustment of pH and density for optimal injection behaviour. Moreover, to ensure stable operation and product quality under these modified conditions, NextSteel integrates a holonic DT (HoloSteel) with advanced PAT for real-time process monitoring and improved process understanding and QbD principles. As such, this digital layer supports real-time monitoring, predictive simulation, and adaptive control of process parameters in response to variability in feedstock and product properties. Importantly, the NextSteel technological solutions are designed for compatibility with both greenfield and brownfield steelmaking contexts to ensure that the innovations can be deployed in newly built plants as well as retrofitted into existing installations, for supporting widespread decarbonisation of the EU steel industry.

The European steel industry is a world leader in innovation and environmental sustainability. It has a turnover of ⁓€170 billion, directly employs 330,000 highly skilled workers, and produces ⁓160 million tonnes of steel per year. With >500 steel production sites across 22 EU Member States, the sector is deeply integrated with Europe’s manufacturing and construction industries, and steel is the backbone for development, growth & employment. The European steel industry has committed to a CO2 reduction of 80–95% by 2050, ensuring that Europe will meet the requirements of the Paris Agreement. However, the reality is that the steel sector is at highest risk of carbon leakage and is the most impacted by unilateral climate policy among energy intensive industries. The cost-effectiveness of climate policy measures and proven technical feasibility of sustainable technologies are vital to avoid the shifting of investment, production, jobs and CO2 emissions out of the EU to third countries with laxer environmental rules. As the reduction potential of the current steelmaking routes are low, the transfer toward breakthrough-technologies such as those being realised in NextSteel is essential to help the EU reach decarbonisation goals. With ⁓10 million tonnes (Mt) of dry sludge and 80+ Mt of food waste produced in Europe every year, about 15 Mt of biogenic carbon is available to be turned alternative to coal for the steel sector. NextSteel’s aims to feed into this market with an integrated HTT–SCWG–MCP biowaste valorisation process for the production of high-performance bioanthracite & green hydrogen for decarbonised steelmaking routes (H₂-based DRI + EAF; EAF and BF with biogenic anthracite; and SiMn production with C–SiO₂-rich bioanthracite). Based on an adjudication and ranking of the low-carbon routes, we will prioritise the candidates for full Business Case development (but the goal is that all validated and viable routes are included), based on: their readiness for adoption (thanks to the validated and industrial demonstration with steelmakers (WP3), supported by AMMR, ERA, CELSA); LCA, techno-economic modelling, and emissions scenarios (WP4), covering CAPEX, OPEX, ROI, and payback times; as well as market level uses, e.g. the DRI+EAF route could be an ideal candidate for first business case development due: strong alignment with EU investment trends, modular scalability within existing industrial infrastructure; Dual input valorisation, using both green hydrogen and bioanthracite from the same upstream process chain; Lower CAPEX compared to BF retrofits and stronger alignment with emerging “green steel” certification schemes. Moreover, market projections estimate that hydrogen-based DRI capacity in Europe could reach 20–30 Mt/year by 2030, representing a ~€40–60 billion investment landscape. The additional process routes (BF/EAF, SiMn) could then be part of subsequent business cases to support diversified market uptake, de-risk deployment & respond to varying plant contexts across EU.
A robust technology adoption framework that will facilitate the post-project investment in the NextSteel technologies and processes in the low carbon steelmaking routes, and will consists of: scaling project demonstrators (from TRL6 at project end to TRL7-TRL8) and industrial scalability roadmap, framing of the cost-effectiveness and expected ROI (Business Case) of the NextSteel processes and innovations (facilitated by modelling, and the real-time process monitoring (in a PAT and QbD approach) and the Digital Twin technology), a biowaste resource map, securing industrial integrators, and user acceptance, skills development package (training materials developed during the project to support demonstration work) and future recommendations, along with a Business Plan (which will benefit from lighthouse industry representatives such as ERA, CELSA, AMMR) and a financing strategy that targets (among others) financial products under the InvestEU Fund for further commercialisation of the NextSteel outcomes. Links with Processes4Planet and Clean Steel will be established from the outset, with a view to preparing for the delivery of a long-term impact of contributing of 15% to the overall European metallurgy target for GHG emissions reduction of 80–95% by 2050.
The exploitation strategy is reinforced by a commitment to protect commercially sensitive IPR “within Europe” and “for Europe,” enabling market exclusivity, investment, licensing, and brand value. The EC recognises IPR as vital to innovation, growth, and competitiveness—accounting for 42% of EU GDP and 80% of exports. This is especially relevant for NextSteel, where innovation drives the transition to low-CO₂ steelmaking. The consortium has already identified high-potential, commercially sensitive innovations with strong licensing and exploitation potential.

b) Scale and significance of the project’s contribution to the expected outcomes and impacts

The European steel industry is one of the most carbon-intensive sectors, emitting approximately 221 MtCO₂ annually, with ~55% of production still based on the BF–BOF route, and ~45% through scrap-based EAF (Worldsteel, 2023; Eurofer, 2024). The transition toward DRI–EAF is accelerating but expected to remain incomplete before 2050 (IEA, 2020). Steelmaking is also a highly complex system, where variability in feedstocks, reductants, and process conditions cascades through the chain, affecting energy use, process kinetics, product quality, and environmental performance. This complexity is heightened in the low-carbon transition, which introduces heterogeneous, bio-based and secondary feedstocks into the mix.
Scale: Crude steel production in the EU continues to rely predominantly on three core production routes: the BF-BOF, EAF, and increasingly, DRI combined with EAF. According to Eurofer’s  report, in 2023, approximately 55% of EU crude steel was produced via the BF-BOF route, and 45% via EAF , with DRI-EAF is expected to grow steadily during the decarbonisation transition. Some projections estimate that by 2030, the EAF share in Europe could rise to around 57%, reflecting a shift toward lower-CO₂ production pathways, but with BF-BOF remaining significant well into the next decades. NextSteel is strategically positioned to deliver impact across all three major steelmaking routes, ensuring relevance and applicability across the entire steel sector. With pilot activities involving 4 industrial players (AMMR, CELSA, ERA, CELSA), the project enables validation and upscaling of solutions in diverse, real-world operational contexts.
Significance: The steel industry is undergoing a phased transformation towards climate neutrality, but transition timelines vary. BF-BOF routes will remain dominant in many EU Member States well into the 2040s. Replacing fossil-based reductants with biogenic alternatives in BF-BOF, EAF, and DRI is crucial to lowering fossil dependence in the medium term, while laying the groundwork for long-term system transition. NEXTSTEEL combines novel pretreatment (fast HTT), carbon valorisation (bioanthracite), and low-cost green gas generation technologies into existing steelmaking pathways, unlocking up to 70% recovery of biogenic carbon from wastes, contributing to circular materials flow and emissions avoidance. Our focus on BF, in addition to EAF is important, in that the steelmaking industry will go through a long transition phase towards EAF-DRI ironmaking process route in Europe (min 2050). In the prevalent BF/BOF route, approximately 1.9 t of CO2 is emitted per tonne of crude steel, encompassing agglomeration, iron- and steelmaking, casting, and hot rolling43. The scrap-based EAF route exhibits a lower carbon footprint (455 kg CO2 t−1CS)44. In terms of the decarbonisation of the industry, replacement of carbon by renewable electricity and/or fossil-free reductants to directly avoid CO2 emissions (i.e. Carbon Direct Avoidance [CDA]) is one (of two) significant routes for achieving substantial CO2-reduction rates in the steel sector (the other being Smart Carbon Usage). At the system level, the integration of the HoloSteel digital intelligence layer provides adaptive process control and predictive insights, enabling steelmakers to handle highly variable, non-fossil inputs without sacrificing product quality or energy performance. This digital capability increases process stability, resilience, and operational tolerance- critical enablers for scaling low-CO₂ pathways. If NextSteel’s innovations are adopted in just 15% of EU steel plants (approx. 50 installations) by 2050, they could enable direct annual GHG savings of up to 24 Mt CO₂, supporting the Net-Zero Industry Act, the Clean Steel Partnership’s roadmap, and broader European decarbonisation and circular economy objectives.
