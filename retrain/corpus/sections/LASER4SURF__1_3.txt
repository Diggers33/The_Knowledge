Objectives 
The main objective of this WP is the development of an easy to integrate and control, compact and low cost optical 
module for the Laser Texturing Prototype. This objective is divided in the following specific objectives:  
- To determine the detailed specifica tions of the optical module required for each application (material and 
functionalization required) to obtain the optimum performance for each one. 
- To define the best beam shape(s) characteristics to cover a broad range of applications.  
- To define the interfaces between the future optical modules and the Laser Texturing Prototype.  
- To develop the optical module(s) to be integrated in WP5 
Description of work and role of partners 
WP2 - DESIGN AND DEVELOPMENT OF A PROTOTYPE FOR CONTROLLED LIPSS  [Months: 1-24] 
MULTITEL, Ceit-IK4, LASEA 
In this WP the consortium (leaded by MULTITEL) will work to develop the optical module of the Laser Texturing 
Prototype. This optical module is the responsible to control the parameters that determine the LIPSS formation: optical 
power, beam profile, polarisation orientation, and even the operation wavelength of the micromachining system.  
Task 2.1 Definition of the System Architecture. 
Lead Partner: MULT - Participant Partners: LASEA, CEIT, VISUM, FAGOR, RESCOLL and CICe - Task duration: 
M01-M06 
The partners involved in this task will work on determining the detailed specifications required for each material/ 
functionalization combination defined for the development of the Prototype, in order to obtain the optimum 
performance in ter ms of LIPSS formation quality and process time for each one. The more relevant specifications 
include: (i) Determination of the minimum and maximum line width, that is the width covered with LIPSS with a 
specific orientation. (ii) Determination of the rang e of energy densities necessary to cover the materials and 
functionalization combinations. (iii) Determination of the beam profile/shape. (iv) Determination of the polarization 
requirements (linear, circular, elliptical,…) (iv) Determination of the laser wavelength. 
Taking into account the specifications defined, MULTITEL and LASEA will make a preliminary definition of the Laser 
and optic architecture needed to adequately cover the range defined. 
Task 2.2 Preparation of the experimental laboratory set-up and tests. 
Lead Partner: MULTITEL - Participant Partners: CEIT, LASEA - Task duration: M01-M15 
In this task, an experimental laboratory set -up will be defined with the aim of testing and defining more precisely the 
parameters of the future optical module(s) defined in Task 2.1. 
The goal will be to define the best beam shape(s) characteristics to cover a broad range of applications (materials and 
functionalities). Top-hat beam shape will be certainly the mostly used but depending on the applications requirements 
like working dista nce, spot size and depth of field, the exact characteristics of the top -hat profile may be adapted. In 
particular, the steepness and the homogeneity of the beam shape are of great importance. To define the best beam 
shape(s), MULTITEL will upgrade its current system. 
The system that MULTITEL currently uses (see figure 13) is based on a milliJoule femtosecond IR source coupled to 
high speed galvanometric (scanning module). It comprises a spatial beam shaping module that includes a beam shaping 
based on a Dif fractive Optical Element (DOE) and a polarization part, which permits to manually orientate the linear 
polarisation state of the beam at the input of the beam shaper (spatial beam shaping module).

part B. 
In order to set the required parameters, it is necessary to address a wide range of optical configurations. Therefore, 
flexibility is the most important parameter regarding the beam shaping method to be used, regardle ss of integration 
losses and handling power. In this regard, to carry out the experimental tests the currently system used by MULTITEL 
will be upgraded to obtain a more flexible system by:

- The implementation of a spatial light modulator (SLM) based polarization and beam shaper for producing 
controlledpolarized beams: linearly, circular, azimuthally and radially polarized beams. An optical arrangement 
including a SLM component will permit a more flexible control of the polarization, not limited to linear state. As for 
beam shaper, the use of use SLMs instead of DOEs will allow to change the beam shape, obtaining a flexible and 
dynamic beam shaping and control. 
- The implementation of multi-beam splitting functions. 
Based on this flexible and dynamic beam shaping and control system, the following experimental test will be carried 
out to define the exact parameters of the optical modules required for achieving the specifications defined in task 
2.1: - First, for each material (Aluminium, cooper, titanium and stainless steels) the beam profile and polarization 
orientation to obtain different ripples will be defined (in terms of depth and periods) by: test of Gaussian, Super 
Gaussian and Tophat with different steepnesses combined with different polarization modes. 
- Then, for each application (materials and functionalities) the best beam profile in terms of depth of focus, 
overlappingand sensitivity to stitching issues will be defined. For instance, to m ake hydrophobic surfaces, it is 
important to be very careful about stitching issues and in this case, a precise overlap control with well -defined 
homogeneous beam is required. 
A series of at least 20 samples of 10* 10 cm² from each material will be used for these tests. 
From the two series of experiments, a trade-off will be defined between optical module impact on process sensitivity. 
Once the exact beam shape parameters have been defined, then for the development of the optical module (task 2.4) 
a passive solution will be preferred for cost and power handling aspects.

Task 2.3 Definition of the optical, electrical and mechanical interfaces of the optical modules 
Lead Partner: LASEA - Participant Partners: MULT, CEIT - Task duration: M15-M18 
The aim of this task is to define the interfaces between the future optical modules and the Laser Texturing Prototype. 
This includes software interface, electrical control, optical properties of the laser source in and out of the optical 
module and mechanical characteristics. 
The different parameters to be defined are: 
- Input beam cha racteristics: Beam size, Gaussian shape, M², spectral bandwidth, power and energy. Some 
requirementsdepend directly on the applications (like ablation thresholds will impose energy) but others are only 
dependant on the quality of the laser source. Although  the optical module must be very robust to input laser 
parameters, some limits will be defined here. These limits will be settled from experimental tests at MULTITEL on 
the influence of the input beam shape on the output shape from the optical module.  
- Mechanical dimensions of the optical module: This module will be integrated in the laser path for the 
galvanometricscanner. The weight, size and fixing/interfacing with the scanner will be defined here. A trade-off will 
have to be found between MULTITEL and LASEA regarding the compactness of the future system. 
- Electrical interfacing: This corresponds mainly to the way that the polarization state will be driven and 
controlled. Theinput and output signals from/to the optical to/from the machine will be also defin ed between 
MULTITEL and LASEA. 
The control panel will be integrated in LASEA global software. 
Outcomes: Interfaces of the optical module. 
Task 2.4 Implementation and test of the optical modules. 
Lead Partner: MULT - Participant Partners: LASEA, CEIT - Task duration: M18-M24 
In this task MULTITEL will construct a single universal optical module, able to address the whole panel of 
applications (materials/functionalities) defined for the project. The objective of the task is to obtain a low cost, 
compact solution, easy to integrate and control. In this case, dynamic flexibility will not be the main parameter for 
what concerns the beam shaping solution. As in most of industrial processes, dynamic beam shape is not required 
and induces extra-costs and can reduce the robustness of the system. If a generic beam profile can be found to cope 
with most of the applications, this will be the preferred industrial solution. 
The aim of this task is to develop a low loss method, able to sustain high power and energy laser op eration and 
robust to the input laser parameters fluctuation like in particular polarisation. Therefore, instead of Dynamic 
solutions, which can involve an important cost for beam shaping, Passive methods are going to be use in this solution. 
Among the dif ferent passive methods, diffractive optics will be probably used for beam shaping because of its 
compactness, robustness and easiness of integration. Nevertheless, if on the preliminary test performed in task 2.2 
it is proven that a dynamic beam shaper is the most sensitive solution to cover the whole panel of applications, then 
at the begging of this task will take place a study about the economic impact of dynamic beam shape, analysing its 
cost against its potential market to determine its use.

The beam s haping element will be combined with a polarization rotating element that will be externally driven for a 
remote control of the beam polarization state. A schematic view of the system can be seen in Appendix I of part B.  
More than one module may be constructed depending on the applications requirements. A maximum of three versions 
will be prepared in order to cover the whole panel of applications defined. If during the definition phase it is identified

Task 3.1 Core optical system development 
Lead Partner: VISUM - Participant Partners: CEIT, LASEA - Task duration: M01-M07 
Using surface textured samples that are available from previous research projects from CEIT and VISUM, VISUM will 
start very early in the project to work on the offline trials with a benchtop diffractometry test rig incorporating different 
illumination sources (Different wavelengths, visible and non -visible light) and polarisations, and compact optical 
designs with high numerical aperture collection optics, to validate the suit able set up for measuring samples, while 
manufacturing process analysis will be advanced in parallel. CEIT and LASEA will use their previously existing setups 
to fabricate and provide samples in order to cover the whole range of all the different material/ functionality 
combinations. This means different LIPSS periods, depths and orientation as well as different materials.  
Tests will be performed in the lab scale in non -continuous punctual mode on a relevant set of samples (between 20 -30 
samples will be anal ysed) for obtaining the textures’ features of interest, on planar and curved surfaces. CEIT and 
VISUM will test the reference samples by electron microscopy and analysed by image analysis, including FFT (Fast 
Fourier Transform) to calibrate the optical sys tems. Where possible, simple and direct correlations will be made 
between structural features and diffracted light patterns, to accelerate the analysis process, for example relating 
diffracted order orientation with the direction nano-scale lines on the surface. 
Outcomes: Identification of the optimum optical technology system to develop

The software is intended for driving the instrument, processing the data by means of predictive models and delivering

The aim of this WP is to develop the necessary tools that enable industrial end -users to select the process parameters

It is important to highlight that, the modelling tool developed in this WP intends to be a general tool that can be upgraded

Task 4.1 Generation of a database of LIPSS processes and resulting functionalities  
Lead Partner: CEIT - Participant Partners: LASEA, MULTITEL, CICe, FAGOR, RESCOLL - Task duration: M1-M24 
The g eneration of LIPSS on metallic materials and their properties depend both on laser process parameters 
(wavelength, energy per pulse, pulse duration, number of accumulated pulses per area, polarization, scanning velocity 
…) and material properties (initial roughness, electric properties, optical properties …). As a result, LIPSS with 
different periods, depths and orientations can be obtained. Therefore, the first step of the software development is the 
generation of database that contains the obtained LIPSS geometries and their related functionalities, together with the 
process parameters used. 
This will require the fabrication of LIPSS on the surface of the target materials (aluminium, copper, titanium and 
stainless steel) as well as a characterization of the resulting geometries and functionalities: 
- First of all, an experimental matrix will be defined for each of the 4 selected materials and functionality pair. 
Eachmatrix will contain at least 7 values for each of the parameters affecting LIPSS formation (en ergy per pulse, 
number of pulses, scanning speed, beam polarization, etc. ) These values will be selected according to end -user 
recommendations for the use cases (CICe, CEGASA, FAGOR, RESCOLL) and according to previous results and 
bibliographic search for the rest of functionalities. A series of 5 samples will be manufactured by CEIT for each 
combination of parameters. - The structural characterization of the resulting surfaces will be done by Scanning electron 
microscopy (SEM) and atomic Force microscopy in order to analyse the period, depth and orientation of the LIPSS by 
CEIT and CICe. A Fourier analysis of the SEM images will enable an accurate determination of the mean period of the 
structures and it will be performed by CEIT. 
- The characterization of those samples specifically related to the project use cases will be evaluated according to 
eachspecific application (batteries, encoders and/or implants) by the partners involved in each of them according to 
their specific procedure s (CICe, Fagor, RESC) in order to assess the resulting functionality. As the functionalization 
for batteries is performed in the current collectors and a number of fabrication steps are need afterwards, the evaluation 
of this particular case requires a deeper analysis. This particular evaluation will be performed as follows: 
The characterization for the Batteries application field will include surface tension and wettability studies (against 
electrode slurry) to be carried out by CEIT and CICe in order to evaluate the coating ability of the slurries on the current 
collector surface. Untreated current collectors will also be characterized in order to evaluate the improvements obtained 
by LIPSS processing against untreated current collectors. CEGASA will define the best suited electrochemical testing

conditions required for the selected electrode materials to proof the concept and will also provide a detailed initial 
anode and cathode formulation and negative to positive mass/capacity ratio. Using this formulation as the starting point, 
CICe will work on the optimisation of electrode formulation with the help of CEGASA. The slurry preparation, 
electrode casting and calendering will be performed at CICe Dry Room facility considering the input provided by 
CEGASA whilst the electrochemical characterisation will require the participation of CICe and CEGASA. Discs will 
be punched from the different laminates for their electrochemical characterization which will be performed at CICe as

Work package title INTEGRATION OF THE DEVELOPED TOOLS

Objectives 
The aim of this task is to develop a system that will merge in one single machine the optical module, inspection unit 
and modelling tool. The specific objectives are the following: 
- To define the specifications at mechanical, optical and electric level of the integrated prototype.  
- To ingrate the three modules developed in the previous WPs into a global system to validate the new concept 
ofmachine proposed in the project. 
- To test the system and validate it. 
Description of work and role of partners 
WP5 - INTEGRATION OF THE DEVELOPED TOOLS [Months: 1-33] 
LASEA, Ceit-IK4, MULTITEL, VISUM, CICe, FAGOR, RESCOLL 
The integration of the different modules will be done using an already existing setup of LASEA. It consists of a granite 
based setup with a gantry. It can be equipped with a femtosecond laser emitting at 1030 nm with pulse durations of 
about 400 fs or with  a nanosecond laser with wavelengths of 1064nm or 532 nm and pulse durations of about 100ns 
down to 15ns, bringing flexibility to the possibilities of the setup. 
In this WP, the specifications for the integration of each module in the current setup will be  defined for the different 
modules, taking into account the current technical aspects of the setup such as mechanical dimensions. This information 
will be gathered and communicated to other partners prior to the building of the modules in order to be taken  into 
account in their designs. 
Due to their roles and technical characteristics, the flexibility in the integration of the different modules will be different 
for each module. For instance, the module developed in WP2 is an inline module and will have the strictest limitations 
in terms of integration, directly related to the already existing configuration such as the dimensions of the granite, the 
gantry, which will bring some limitations on the possibilities of change on the mechanical parts such as the p osition of 
mirrors mounts and so, limitations on the change of the optical beam path. Regarding the monitoring module developed 
in WP3, those restrictions are less strict as this module is off beam path, and so there is more flexibility to integrate it 
on the setup, but still, in accordance with the configuration of the setup and the dimensions of the module, its location 
will be chosen depending on mechanics and optics aspects that will have to be defined accurately.  
Those difficulties will be overcome thanks to a close collaboration between LASEA and the different partners involved 
in the WP2, WP3 and WP4.

Work package title TECHNOLOGY VALIDATION IN THREE FIELDS  
Start month 18 End month  36 
Objectives 
The aim of this WP is to demonstrate the feasibility of the proposed laser texturing prototype in a set of 3 products of 
different sectors: Machine tool industry, healthcare sector and batteries. The specific objectives are the following:  
- To obtain a current collector with enhanced adhesion and roughening using the laser texturing prototype - 
To obtain a dental implant with adhesion and osteointegration properties using the laser texturing prototype 
- To obtain an optical encoder with enhanced optical properties of reflection. 
Description of work and role of partners 
WP6 - TECHNOLOGY VALIDATION IN THREE FIELDS [Months: 18-36] 
FAGOR, Ceit-IK4, LASEA, CICe, RESCOLL 
Each of the task detailed below includes the specific activities that will be necessary to carry out to validate the product 
surface functionalisation on each use case. 
It is important to highlight that the samples will be manufactured in LASEA premises and will be sent to the 
corresponding use cases partner who will carried out the necessary test to validate the functionalisation obtained. At 
least two sets of samples will be fabricated for each use case, although additional sets can be manufactured if necessary 
for the functionality validation tasks. The results of the different tests that will be performed in each task will be transfer 
to WP5, in order to analyse them and take into consideration any possible changes that would be required to guarantee 
the feasibility of the prototype. 
Task 6.1 Technology Validation in Energy Storage. 
Lead Partner: CICe - Participant Partners: CEGASA (third party), LASEA - Task duration: M18-M36 
Subtask 6.1.1 Fabrication of structured current collectors for pouch cell prototypes  
Lead Partner: CICe - Participant Partners: LASEA, CEGASA - Task duration: M18-M33 
In this task LASEA will fabricate structured current collectors from commercial rolls used in battery industry following 
input from CICe and CEGASA. The total structured surface will allow for the fabrication of a battery prototype in 
pouch cell format. 
Subtask 6.1.2 Monolayer pouch cell fabrication and electrochemical characterization . 
Lead Partner: CICe - Participant Partners: CEGASA (third party) - Task duration: M22-M33 
CICe will prepare monolayer pouch cells using electrodes which will be prepared using the structured current collector 
and also non-structured as reference. For this several parts of CICe’s prototyping line will have to be adjusted. Coated 
area will be increased by a factor of ca. 20, which will represent a significant increase in the capacity delivered by the 
cell. Pouch cells are one of the different cell designs which are commercially available together with the cylindrical and 
prismatic configurations. Building pouch cells involves processing steps different from those to build coin cells such 
as rectangular electrode notching, ultrasonic welding, pouch sealing, degassing… which are common processing steps 
for commercial battery fabrication. Therefore, building a monolayer pouch cell will constitute a step ahead towards the 
fabrication of cells with standardized configuration and processing techniques to which it wi ll be a closer comparative 
in terms of capacity and processing. 
Subtask 6.1.3 Characterisation of pouch cell electrodes 
Lead Partner: CICe - Participant Partners: CEGASA (third party) - Task duration: M31-M36 
The electrodes to be used for the pouch cells w ill be initially characterised to ensure they meet the required standard 
and also to check reproducibility from coin cell up to pouch cell level. In addition, cycled pouch cells will be 
disassembled after use so as to study the electrode evolution. This characterisation will include visual inspection of the 
electrode and also some advanced characterisation such as SEM and XPS.  
Outcomes: Current collector with enhanced adhesion and roughening, leading to:  
- Improvement of a 30% in the life cycle of battery electrodes, compared to electrodes casted on conventional 
currentcollectors. 
- Increase the battery C-rate capability by 20% when cycling at 1C and by 60% when cycling at 2C.

precision achieved with the grating. CEIT will correlate the gratin properties with the surface LIPSS.  
Outcomes: Optical encoders with enhanced optical properties for reflection, leading to:  
- To increase twofold the tolerances of distances between the grating and the optical head 
- To eliminate the background light of the gratings

Task 8.1. Exploitation 
Lead Partner: LASEA - Participant Partners: CEIT, MULT, VISUM, FAGOR, RESCOLL, CIC - Task duration: M7- 
M36 
The task will implement a specific methodology provided by the partner (LASEA) based on a series of specific tasks 
that will enable the optimization of the exploitation possibilities of the project results. 
Subtask 8.1.1 Identification of key results and functional analysis of each key result. (LASEA, CEIT, MULTITEL,  
VISUM, FAGOR, RESCOLL, CIC) 
This task will carry out a series of workshops aimed at i dentifying the most promising results for exploitation. The 
project outputs with the greatest potential for exploitation are already identified at this proposal stage, but the workshops 
will make the revision of these expected outputs adjusting them (if ne cessary) to the reality of the R&D being 
performed, and already keeping in mind the exploitation objectives of the running project.  
After the identification of the key results, the task will carry out a functional analysis of each of the identified results . 
This functional analysis will identify what is the characteristic or ability of the different outputs to meet the end users’ 
needs according to their feedback (the presence in the consortium of industrial partners such as RESCOLL, FAGOR 
and CEGASA (Third party of CICe) will help). The reason for making this analysis is to ensure that the project delivers 
results that match the purpose for which they have been designed, adequately answering the needs of the end users. The 
task will identify and characterise the functions that the project outputs should offer to meet the needs of the users.  
Subtask 8.1.2: Value innovation analysis and market analysis. (LASEA) 
After the functional analysis of the key results, a value innovation analysis will be carried out, which means identifying 
the features, benefits and advantages of the key outputs. The objective of the project is to provide a product that 
maximizes these advantages to be compelling to the end user. Moreover, the characteristics of the product should make 
it competitively distinctive, so that the commercial exploitation possibilities are maximized. According to this, the value 
innovation analysis will try to set the stage for answering the next question “How to make the project outputs 
significantly unique or competitively advantageous?” 
Within the task, the real value of the expected outputs for the users will be examined in detail. It is necessary to compare 
the value of the project outputs with the potential market competitors, and this is why a market analysis will be carried 
out. In the framework of this market analysis, the following subtasks will be considered: Analysis of the potential 
customer demands, Identification and characterisation of the potential customers, why and how they would buy the 
product or service?; Market size, trends and opportunities; Analyse competing solutions; What are the competing 
solutions available on the market today?; How well do they match the functional requirements that we have identified?; 
What is their customer price? 
Subtask 8.1.3: Value proposition and value proposition testing. (LASEA, CEIT, MULT, VISUM, FAGOR, RESCOLL, 
CICe)

Once the value innovation analysis has been completed, the next task will be to make a new innovation value proposition 
for the expected project outputs, which optimizes the value of the project outputs in comparison with potential 
competitors. This means building and proposing a “compelling” and competitively distinctive value curve so that we 
make sure that the project outputs stand out of the competition while still being outputs achievable within the project 
scope. 
The value proposition will be built with the objective of making the project outputs significantly unique or 
competitively advantageous. For this, four directions will be explored for making the value innovation proposal:  
- To cancel value: Which of the value components that competitors take for granted should be eliminated?  
- To reduce value: Which value components can be reduced below the actual standards of industry?  
- To increase value: Which value components must be increased above the actual standards or industry?- Which 
value components, that competitors have never offered, should be created?  
Within this task, it will be decided which combination of the described directions will be taken in order to make the 
project value innovation proposition. 
Once the va lue innovation proposition has been established, this will be tested with potential end users or early 
adopters to assure that it will answer their needs and expectations. This will be accomplished through interviews and 
questionnaires to a set of identifi ed potential end users. The result of this testing will be fed -back to the value 
proposition in order to tune it according to the end users’ requirements. 
Subtask 8.1.4: IPR analysis of the new knowledge and project outputs. (LASEA, CEIT, MULT, VISUM, FAGO R, 
RESCOLL, CIC) 
This task will deal with the IPR issues related to the project exploitation, and includes: (i) The definition of pre -
existing knowledge of the project partners (Background), including a mapping of existing patents and potentially 
overlapping IPR; (ii) An assessment of the knowledge generated in the project (Foreground). (ii) A proposition of 
optimal IPR protection options, in line with the selected business model options (to be selected in the next task); (iii) 
A mapping of existing relevan t standards and of standards in development; (iv) Considerations with regards to the 
standardisation strategy (e.g. get involved in on -going standardisation technical committees or develop a new 
standard…) 
Subtask 8.1.5: Development of business models, bus iness plans and exploitation action plans. (LASEA, CEIT, 
MULT, VISUM, FAGOR, RESCOLL, CIC) 
Within this task the different business options for the project results will be investigated, establishing the preliminary 
business models. These potential business  model options may include: Take -up by one of the partners (or a joint -
venture of partners) and commercialisation of the output by this partner with adequate distribution channels; 
Patenting of the output with third -party licensing; Direct commercialisation of the output; Integration of the output 
in another solution. According to the selected business models, the task will build the exploitation action plans for 
each of the results of the project. The structure of the exploitation plans will include: The c onsortium and the 
opportunity; The solution and value proposition; Market and competition analysis; Business model options and 
marketing plan (distribution architecture / sales channels, customer relationship); Resources for implementing 
exploitation (key activities and value chain, key capabilities, partners); Financial planning (cost and revenues); 
Timeline; Risk management and contingencies; IPR strategy + Standardisation strategy.

Subtask 8.2.3. Creation of original content (ESCI, LASEA, CEIT, MULTITEL, VISUM, FAGOR, RESCOLL, CIC) 
A variety of editorial and visual content will be developed to share on LASER4SURF channels, media multipliers and 
social media. By focusing on the message, not the  medium we will help create a credible, sustainable interest for

experts, end users and stakeholders) will be interviewed and a total of 12 interviews will be produced.  
- Original journalistic articles: As LASER4SURF gains momentum, a series of six original journalistic articles 
willprofile the skills, experiences, credibility and performance of the demonstration use cases and project in more 
detail. - Info-graphics: six info -graphics demonstrating key concepts, interoperability issues and results will be 
prepared. These are highly u seful in many scenarios, including twitter, presentations, postcard flyers and posters. 
An initial series of three info-graphics focused on key concepts of the project will be defined to help attract audiences 
and build on the first social media actions. A further three will be produced towards the end of the project to profile 
key results. 
- Brochure: A project’s brochure will be designed in M6 in order to provide additional information on printed 
materialfor fairs and conferences. 
- Audiovisual products: Two audio-visual products will be produced in professional HD broadcast standard to 
promotethe project. First, a short, concise and creative introductory video using animation will be developed within 
the first four months and will be used to capture and keep the attention of targeted audiences to stay informed about 
the project. The second one (produced at the end of the project) will show the results of the project, becoming an 
important tool also for the exploitation of project’s results. 
- Peer-reviewed scien tific papers and poster presentation: different peer -reviewed scientific papers will be 
preparedduring the project lifetime to promote project results, as well as technical papers or poster presentations (see 
section 2.3 for further details). ESCI will support the LASER4SURF partners in this task, in particular in the creation 
of compelling poster presentations, and will use their online channels to disseminate these publications to the 
relevant target groups. Open access channels and platforms such as Rese archgate will be used to support 
transparency and share insight. 
Outcomes: Production of different editorial and visual content to share on LASER4SURF channels, media 
multipliers and social media.
