=== Domain B: photonic sensors for pharmaceutical inline process analytical technology (PAT) ===
=== Section 3_3: Consortium as a whole ===

2.3. Envisioned impact 2.3.1. Expected impacts listed in the work programme Our proposal will contribute to the following expected impacts:  
• It will help close the gap between research and market introduction of innovative process analytical technologies (PAT) for in-line and on-line monitoring and control of pharmaceutical processes through the development of a novel PAT system based on photonic sensors, integrated in a smart miniaturized microfluidic platform, and implemented in an industrial environment.  
• It will enable innovation in the pharmaceutical manufacturing sector by providing a novel PAT solution for in-line and on-line monitoring and control of pharmaceutical processes, which will significantly improve the efficiency of the manufacturing process.  
• It will lead to the development of a new and improved monitoring and control technology based on photonic sensors for pharmaceutical manufacturing that is more accurate, reliable, and cost-effective than existing technologies.  
• It will stimulate innovation in the pharmaceutical manufacturing sector by providing a novel PAT solution for in-line and on-line monitoring and control of pharmaceutical processes, which will enable faster and more efficient drug development.  
• It will foster the emergence of new business models in the pharmaceutical manufacturing sector by providing a novel PAT solution for in-line and on-line monitoring and control of pharmaceutical processes, which will enable improved product quality and reduced production costs.  
• It will contribute to the enhancement of the competitiveness of European industry by providing a novel PAT solution for in-line and on-line monitoring and control of pharmaceutical processes, which will enable the development of new and improved pharmaceutical products.  
• It will contribute to the strengthening of the European Research Area (ERA) by providing a novel PAT solution for in-line and on-line monitoring and control of pharmaceutical processes, which will enable faster and more efficient drug development and production.  
• It will encourage the emergence of new business models in the pharmaceutical manufacturing sector by delivering a novel PAT solution for in-line and on-line monitoring and control of pharmaceutical processes, enabling improved product quality and reduced production costs.
