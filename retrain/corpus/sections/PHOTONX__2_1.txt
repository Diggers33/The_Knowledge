2.1 Project’s pathways towards impact

a) Unique contribution of project results to (1) the outcomes specified in this topic, and (2) the wider impacts, in the longer term, specified in the destinations in the work programme

PhotonX makes advanced photonics and multimodal sensing more accessible as key enabling technologies for Europe’s sustainable, digital, and sovereign future. It delivers affordable and easy-to-use sensing that combines precision, versatility, and intelligent integration to improve efficiency, reliability, and decision-making in industry, while cutting resource use and operational costs. Thanks to strengthen the innovation of European technology players from the photonics supply chain (ICL/QCL- ALPES, SPAD imaging- NV (a newcomer to Horizon Europe), photonics integrator- IRIS) the project makes a solid contribution to strengthens Europe’s open strategic autonomy by reducing dependence on external suppliers in critical sensing technologies. By giving industries (such as bioprocessing- MyB, plastics recycling- VW, and steelmaking- CELSA), access to new multimodal tools for increasing their efficiencies and sustainability, thanks to actionable, reliable data, information and predictions, PhotonX supports compliance, competitiveness, and resilience across important European industrial sectors, directly contributing to the green and digital transitions.
(1) Contribution to the Expected Outcomes: PhotonX will make a tangible contribution to the expected outcomes specified in the HORIZON-CL4-2025-04-DIGITAL-EMERGING-01 call topic, as follows:
Increase the efficiency of developed photonic sensors, surpassing existing technologies in aspects such as energy consumption, data acquisition and processing speed, as well as measurement accuracy: PhotonX improves efficiency at both module and system level. The compact ICL/QCL MIR module is expected to cut power consumption by ~30–40% compared to current laboratory-scale MIR systems, while maintaining or extending spectral coverage. The SERS module will deliver up to 10× enhancement in sensitivity versus conventional SERS, reducing acquisition times from seconds to sub-second levels. The SPAD camera with on-chip processing can reduce data transfer and processing latency by 50–70%, enabling real-time analysis at multi-kHz frame rates. By fusing modalities at the physics and signal level, PhotonX reduces redundant measurements and increases effective information yield per photon captured, leading to a projected 20–30% improvement in accuracy, repeatability, and robustness in complex industrial environments. Together, these advances represent step changes over existing bulky, energy-intensive, and single-purpose photonic systems.
Significantly reduce the size, weight, or footprint of the sensors and quantify advancements over current technologies:  PhotonX tackles the footprint challenge through chip-level integration, modular co-design, and miniaturisation. The compact QCL source reduces MIR sensing modules from benchtop units (~20–30 kg, >0.5 m² footprint) to portable modules under 5 kg and <0.05 m². The SPAD camera with on-chip processing eliminates the need for external processing racks, reducing system weight by ~60% and footprint by ~70%. SERS modules built on nanostructured substrates reduce the need for bulky optics, cutting package size by ~50%. By co-integrating sensing heads and enabling shared sampling geometries, PhotonX projects an overall system-level reduction of ~40–60% in size and weight compared to current multimodal setups that combine discrete instruments. These reductions will be validated in the three pilots, where industrial-grade TRL6 (ending in TRL7) modules will replace current benchtop instruments. This miniaturisation not only enables easier integration into industrial lines (steel, plastics, bioprocessing) but also opens pathways to portable, field-deployable, and scalable sensing platforms for additional domains (food, environment, security).
Improve both intrinsic and extrinsic performance metrics to boost measurement accuracy for at least three use-case scenarios: PhotonX boosts both intrinsic (sensitivity, limit of detection, selectivity) and extrinsic (robustness, stability, applicability in harsh conditions) performance across the 3 industrial pilots:
■ Bioprocessing (pharma): MIR ATR + CSA + SERS + SPAD fusion is expected to lower LoD for critical metabolites and the target antibiotic (oxytetracycline) by 1 order of magnitude (µM → sub-µM or nM range), improve measurement repeatability by ~20%, and cut analysis time from minutes to <30s. SPAD adds visual inspection in SWIR (1100 nm) for macroscopic biofilm/heterogeneity detection, enabling more reliable real-time bioprocess control.
■ Steelmaking: CSA+ MIR + Raman + SPAD fusion characterises slag composition, off-gases, and fume contaminants with ~30% improved precision compared to single techniques, while operating robustly at >1,200°C. Extrinsic robustness will be validated with stable uptime >95% under harsh EAF conditions, and SPAD imaging adds anomaly detection for alloy charge control.
■ Plastics recycling: MIR + CSA + SERS + LIBS fusion enables classification and contaminant detection, improving polymer sorting accuracy from ~85% (current SoA) to >95% and reducing false positives by ~40%. LIBS contributes heavy metal and bromine/cadmium detection, while SERS enhances trace detection of PFAS and stabilisers.

These quantifiable gains show that PhotonX can significantly outperform single-modality sensing systems in real-world industrial conditions, where both intrinsic measurement fidelity and extrinsic robustness are critical for adoption.
Extend the sensing and testing capabilities of photonic sensors by linking optical with non-optical measurement parameters (e.g. for acoustic sensing or electromagnetic sensing). PhotonX extends the scope of photonic sensors by fusing optical modalities (MIR, SERS, SPAD imaging, Raman/HSI, LIBS) with non-optical parameters captured through chemical sensor arrays (CSA)/electronic noses (VOC profiles, gas composition), as well as through auxiliary channels such as temperature, acoustic vibration, or electromagnetic interference. This linkage allows the system to capture multi-physics & multi-chemical signatures that single photonic modalities cannot resolve entirely on their own.
In biopharmaceutical processing (UC1), combining MIR (ATR) and SERS spectra with CSA profiles of volatile by-products strengthens monitoring of culture states, enabling early detection of contamination, metabolic shifts, and product formation kinetics that purely optical sensors would miss.
In plastics recycling (UC2), MIR and SERS identification of polymers and additives linked with CSA detection of VOCs captures both material composition and contaminant emissions, improving classification accuracy, compliance with REACH/POP limits, and real-time sorting efficiency.
In steelmaking (UC3), MIR/chemosensors off-gas analysis fused with Raman slag chemistry and SPAD imaging of molten dynamics provides both solid and gaseous process signatures, improving furnace control, early contaminant detection, and safety margins.
The result is a broader and more integrated detection envelope, covering chemical, physical, and environmental parameters in one configurable platform. PhotonX projects a 25-40% increase in detectable parameter space and up to 30% higher measurement robustness compared to photonic-only systems, giving operators a holistic, real-time process view essential for adoption in complex industrial environments.
Contribute to reducing manufacturing costs and increasing resource-efficiency, while also enhancing reliability and durability of the targeted sensor systems: Contribute to reducing manufacturing costs and increasing resource-efficiency, while also enhancing reliability and durability of the targeted sensor systems: PhotonX replaces multiple discrete instruments with a single configurable multimodal platform. Deploying MIR, SERS/Raman, LIBS, SPAD, and chemical sensor arrays today often requires 3–5 standalone devices with a combined cost exceeding €300k; PhotonX is projected to be able to deliver equivalent or superior capability at 30–40% reduction in system-level costs.
Compact QCL/ICL sources and SPAD modules with on-chip processing reduce component count by ~25–35%, while shared optics, common gas-sampling interfaces, and physics-level fusion lower material demand by ~20%. Industrial-grade packaging, ruggedised optics, and SSbD-informed material choices target uptime >95% in harsh environments (vs ~80–85% for lab-based systems). In addition, self-calibration routines and cross-modal drift correction reduce recalibration needs by ~30% and extend mean time between failure (MTBF) beyond 10,000 hours.
This combination of lower capital expenditure, reduced material intensity, and improved lifetime performance ensures that PhotonX delivers resource-efficient, durable, and cost-effective sensor systems, fully aligned with EU sustainability and circularity objectives.
Help secure the open strategic autonomy for Europe by ensuring intellectual property and production means of key technologies are maintained within the EU, reducing dependencies and enhancing negotiation power in technological cooperation. PhotonX is structured to keep core IP and manufacturing inside the EU across photonics and sensing. Key hardware KERs, i.e. compact MIR QCL modules (ALPES), SERS modules, InGaAs SPAD camera with on-chip processing (NV), rugged photonic packaging (HS), and the PhotonX multimodal platform (IRIS), are all developed and industrialised by EU-based SMEs with EU supply chains for critical subcomponents wherever feasible. ICP will lead an EU-first IP strategy (patents, trade secrets, defensive publications), while TEMA will work with the consortium in designing EU manufacturing/business models. UNE will support by early definition of standards to ensure interoperability. Data and software assets follow EU data sovereignty principles (EU hosting options, export-controlled modules ring-fenced, OSS where appropriate with EU-friendly licenses). This will result in reduced dependency on non-EU vendors, stronger EU bargaining position in tech cooperation, and faster industrial uptake within Europe.
Regarding intellectual property, the EC has highlighted IPR as crucial for fostering innovation, competitiveness and economic growth in Europe, noting that that IPR-intensive activities account for 42% of EU GDP, generate 29.7% of total EU employment, and contribute to 80% of EU good & services exports. Indeed, strengthening IPR protection is seen as key to creating jobs, boosting growth, and maintaining Europe’s global leadership. In PhotonX, we are committed to protecting commercially strategic/sensitive IPR “within Europe” and “for Europe” enabling competitive market exclusivity, attracting investment, creating licensing opportunities, and strengthen brand identity- all of which drive business growth and market success in the European photonics and sensing industry. As a strategic asset in our consortium, we have included a specialised partner ICP, to lead the team in the management of intellectual property (IP) for adequately protecting innovative project outcomes where new knowledge is created, ensuring their commercial exploitation and our target, is to secure ≥3-5 patents. This approach aligns with the EC’s emphasis on strengthening IP protection for strategic and commercially sensitive innovations from EU funded R&I projects,,, as reflected in EC communications, annual IPR dashboards (Fig. 8), the IPR Enforcement Action Plan, and Digital Single Market strategies, and recently enacted tenders that promote the objective of boosting IPR and commercialisation from research results. Building on this solid technology and IP foundation, PhotonX will make effective contributions to scientific, economic & societal impacts (discussed below).
Help maximise international competitiveness by increasing the uptake and translation of photonic technologies into new products and services, guided by key technology requirements. PhotonX accelerates the uptake of photonic technologies by delivering industrial-grade modules (photonics: MIR QCL, SERS, SPAD; non-optical: chemical sensor arrays) and a configurable multimodal integration platform developed around explicit end-user requirements from bioprocessing, steelmaking, and plastics recycling pilots. Each module is designed for manufacturability, interoperability, and cost-performance balance, ensuring rapid translation into deployable products and services across multiple industrial domains. Co-creation with industrial partners ensures outputs align with operational needs and market demand, reducing the gap between lab innovation and industrial adoption.
PhotonX targets the creation of a minimum of 5 market-ready exploitable products/services (the 4 modules + integration platform), with validated TRL7 prototypes. Adoption readiness is supported by 3 first-adopter industrial partners in the consortium, with ≥3 additional EU industrial stakeholders engaged through demonstrations and workshops by project end. We also have included technology transfer pathways in T4.2, and we have also identified the potential application pipelines in Table 7 (PhotonX Application Transferability Matrix).
Beyond the three pilots, PhotonX demonstrates transferability into food, agriculture, environment, and security, supported by open validation datasets, standardisation inputs, and training resources. By aligning development with key technology requirements (reduced size/weight, 30–40% lower costs, >95% uptime, interoperability with EU digital frameworks), PhotonX contributes directly to EU sovereignty and strengthens the competitive position of EU SMEs (ALPES, NV, IRIS in photonics; JLM in non-optical sensors), enhancing their IP portfolios and enabling them to scale from niche suppliers to global solution providers.
(2) Contribute to the wider impacts, in the longer term, specified in the work programme destinations:
PhotonX directly contributes to the wider, longer-term impacts specified in Destination 4 “Achieving open strategic autonomy in digital and emerging enabling technologies” by tackling chip-level integration, hybrid sensing paradigms, AI-driven multimodal analytics, industrial efficiency, sustainability, and EU technological sovereignty.
Its most important impact is to secure European sovereignty in photonics-based sensing and multimodal integration, an area currently dependent on non-EU suppliers for critical modules (e.g. ICL/QCL MIR, Raman SERs, SPAD detectors). By developing a fully European sensing stack covering advanced photonic modules, complementary modalities, and a configurable AI-driven multimodal platform, PhotonX ensures that the intellectual property, manufacturing know-how, and production capacity remain within Europe. This reduces dependency, strengthens EU supply chains, and enhances Europe’s negotiation power in global technology markets.
Scientific impact: PhotonX will bring advances in photonics and multimodal sensing fusion (at physics, signal, and data levels), going beyond SOTA, generating new knowledge and methodologies. Securing IP will encapsulate the value of this knowledge for EU opening up new R&D, tech transfer and licensing opportunities. Target groups: research institutions, applied science labs, and standardisation bodies.
Economic/technological impact: PhotonX produces 5 exploitable products/services owned and manufactured in Europe, supporting SMEs in photonics and AI to scale into global markets. PhotonX is also anchored in real industrial deployments (bioprocessing, steelmaking, plastics recycling) and is codesigned with operators and end-users, ensuring adoption and measurable impact, whereby the industrial pilots demonstrate clear efficiency gains (discussed in scale and significance). Target groups: EU technology SMEs, industrial manufacturers, digital service providers.
Societal impact: PhotonX contributes to sustainability and resilience by reducing waste and CO₂ emissions in steelmaking, supporting circular economy practices in recycling, and enabling safer, more efficient pharmaceutical production. Potential environmental footprint from module development (rare materials, production energy use) will be mitigated via eco-design, SSbD, ensuring no significant harm under EU Taxonomy criteria. It empowers operators through upskilling (>200 trained, with ≥40% female participation), ensuring a just and inclusive digital transition, and will open new high value opportunities in a growing and strengthen EU Photonics industry, preventing as well ‘brain drain’ of talented young graduates from Europe. Target groups: industrial workers, citizens regulators, policymakers, and local communities affected by industrial environmental impacts. b) Scale and significance of the project’s contribution to the expected outcomes and impacts

The global photonics market size was valued at USD 920.56 billion in 2023 and is projected to grow from USD 983.51 billion in 2024 to USD 1,642.61 billion by 2032 at a CAGR of 6.7% during the 2024-2032 period. Market growth will be driven by the increasing use of photonics-enabled devices in the healthcare, information and communication, and industrial production sectors. Asia Pacific dominated the photonics market with a market share of 63.2% in 2023. The IR gas sensor market is projected to more than double to $288 million by 2026, from an estimated $132 million in 2021. PhotonX can contribute directly to the innovation capacity and IP assets of European photonics SMEs (ALPES, NV, IRIS) and complementary non-optical sensor providers (JLM), positioning them to gain market share through novel multimodal systems that differentiate Europe in high-value industrial sensing applications. It can also activate a European supply chain for underlying parts and components, reinforcing strategic autonomy and reducing reliance on imports.
PhotonX targets large, strategically very important industrial and societal sectors, addressing the needs of industries and domains that together represent hundreds of billions of euros annually in Europe.
The global biopharmaceutical industry (target of UC1) was worth an estimated at USD 452.21 billion in 2024 and is projected to reach USD 740.84 billion by 2030, growing at a CAGR of 8.87%. This growth is driven by the increasing demand for targeted therapies, advancements in biotechnology, rising prevalence of chronic diseases, and an aging population. PhotonX directly addresses a critical bottleneck in this industry by enabling real-time, non-invasive monitoring of bioprocesses, improving product quality, reducing batch failures, and accelerating time-to-market for advanced therapies. Its multimodal sensing approach will strengthen process analytical technologies (PAT) and support regulatory compliance under FDA and EMA guidelines for biomanufacturing. The first focus on the fermentative production of oxytetracycline (OTC) and related antibiotics, contributes directly to the EU Critical Medicines Act, helping reduce Europe’s dependency on imports and mitigating the risk of shortages. Furthermore, by enabling the sustainable use of renewable feedstocks and minimising wastewater contamination, it aligns with the EU Strategic Approach to Pharmaceuticals in the Environment.
Plastics recycling is an industry worth >€9.1 billion in Europe, with 13.2 million tonnes of installed capacity across ⁓850 recycling facilities (source: Plastics Recyclers Europe) and VW is a leading European company in this sector. Due to the impacts of cheap imported plastics, tough legislation and regulation of EU recycled plastics, high energy costs, among others, by the end of 2025 it is estimated that the region will have lost recycling plants, amounting to a staggering 1 million tonnes of recycling capacity. The Plastics Recyclers Europe points to enhanced monitoring systems as being urgently needed. PhotonX has the potential to directly address this critical gap by enabling real-time, in-line detection of restricted substances and legacy additives, unlocking new value from difficult-to-recycle streams and helping recyclers meet quality standards more efficiently and cost-effectively. Plastics Recyclers Europe outlines the urgent need to ‘enhance monitoring systems to ensure compliance with recycled content targets and that recycled plastics meet the high standards required for critical applications’ If we consider the ambitious targets of VinylPlus 2030, which is the current 10-year Commitment of the European PVC industry to Sustainable Development, to achieve ≥1 million tonnes/year of recycled PVC used in new products by 2030, and to foster science-based solutions for the safe and sustainable use of additives (such as innovative sorting technology), the PhotonX UC2 clearly addresses a recognised and urgent market demand. Beyond regulatory compliance, PhotonX provides recyclers with the tools to maintain competitiveness under high energy costs, reduce dependency on imports, and strengthen Europe’s leadership in sustainable plastics recycling. By improving detection and sorting efficiency by even 10–15%, PhotonX could help recover an additional 100,000-150,000 tonnes of recyclate annually across Europe, i.e. equivalent to avoiding ~200,000-300,000 tonnes of CO₂ emissions and generating €120-180 million in added value for the recycling market each year.
The European steel industry (UC3) is a world leader in innovation and environmental sustainability. It has a turnover of ⁓€170 billion, directly employs 330,000 highly skilled workers, and produces ⁓160 million tonnes of steel per year. With >500 steel production sites across 22 EU Member States, the sector is deeply integrated with Europe’s manufacturing and construction industries, and steel is the backbone for development, growth & employment. PhotonX will enable advanced in-line monitoring of emissions, slag chemistry, and process efficiency in this critical sector, directly supporting its decarbonisation roadmap and competitiveness in global markets. By providing sub-second insights into combustion gases, contaminants, and alloy dynamics, PhotonX would contribute to reducing energy consumption by up to 5-10%/tonne of steel & lowering off-spec batches by 20-30%. This would strengthen EU’s ability to maintain high-quality, low-carbon steel production at scale while ensuring compliance with tightening EU emissions standards & safeguard strategic autonomy in a sector vital for Green Deal & Net-Zero Industry Act.
c) Requirements & barriers that may determine whether the outcomes and impacts are achieved
The achievement of PhotonX’s outcomes and impacts depends on addressing several technical, organisational, and external requirements, while proactively managing potential barriers. PhotonX brings together advanced photonics, complementary sensing modalities, artificial intelligence, and industrial systems integration- positioning itself at the forefront of a multidisciplinary and rapidly evolving technological landscape that underpins Europe’s transition towards sustainable, intelligent, and resilient industries,. We are very well positioned with EPIC and TEMATYS (in T4.1, T4.2) to analyse the requirements and barriers and build our response/action/mitigation strategies. Key enabling conditions include technological readiness, supply chain resilience, regulatory alignment, data governance, and skilled workforce availability. External factors such as economic cycles, materials constraints, evolving standards, or societal acceptance could also influence adoption and impact.
