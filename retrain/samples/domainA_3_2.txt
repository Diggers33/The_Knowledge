2.3 Capacity of participants and consortium as a whole  
  
2.3.1 Relevant experience, skills and infrastructure  
  
Our consortium is a balanced mix of equipment manufacturers (Polytec, Photon Systems), manufacturers of spectroscopic systems (InnospeXion, NIR4.0, IRIS), system integrators (IPT, EBC), end-users (Baader, Lysfjord, VIV, Poligrow), and research organizations (FZJ, UNIVPM, UoG, ICG). Our consortium is composed of 15 partners from 7 different EU countries, with highly complementary expertise, covering the entire value chain from research, design and development to training and dissemination. The Main activities of the partners are described below:  
The University of Portsmouth (UoP) has research expertise in developing machine learning algorithms for real-time food quality control using spectroscopic methods. In addition, UoP has expertise in the development of virtual assistants for the food industry and will lead the development of the virtual assistant in this project.  
Fraunhofer Institute for Laser Technology ILT (ILT) has many years of experience in the development of NIR and MIR laser-based process analytical spectroscopy systems for industrial applications, e.g. in the food sector. ILT will lead the development of the smart sensors and will ensure their integration into the monitoring and control systems of the processing lines.  
Fraunhofer Institute for Industrial Mathematics ITWM (ITWM) has expertise in the development of mathematical models and analysis tools for industrial processes. ITWM will develop the process optimization tools and will ensure their integration into the control systems of the processing lines.  
Fraunhofer Institute for Factory Operation and Automation IFF (IFF) has expertise in the development of virtual assistants for industrial applications. IFF will lead the development of the virtual assistant for the food industry and will ensure its integration into the monitoring and control systems of the processing lines.  
Fraunhofer Institute for Optronics, System Technologies and Image Exploitation (IOSB) has expertise in developing NIR and MIR laser-based process analytical spectroscopy systems for industrial applications, e.g. in the food sector. IOSB will develop the smart sensors and will ensure their integration into the monitoring and control systems of the processing lines.  
InnospeXion A/S (ISX) has expertise in developing NIR and MIR laser-based process analytical spectroscopy systems for industrial applications, e.g. in the food sector. ISX will develop the smart sensors and will ensure their integration into the monitoring and control systems of the processing lines.  
IPT International Packaging Technologies GmbH (IPT) has expertise in the design and development of packaging systems for the food industry. IPT will lead the development of the packaging systems and will ensure their integration with the monitoring and control systems of the processing lines.  
IRIS Technology Solutions (IRIS) has expertise in the development of NIR and MIR laser-based process analytical spectroscopy systems for industrial applications, e.g. in the food sector. IRIS will develop the smart sensors and will ensure their integration into the monitoring and control systems of the processing lines.  
Lysfjord AS (LYS) is a manufacturer of food products. LYS will provide the processing lines for the development and testing of the smart sensors and virtual assistant.  
Poligrow (POL) is a manufacturer of food products. POL will provide the processing lines for the development and testing of the smart sensors and virtual assistant.  
VIV (VIV) is a manufacturer of food products. VIV will provide the processing lines for the development and testing of the smart sensors and virtual assistant.  
Baader (BAA) is a manufacturer of food products. BAA will provide the processing lines for the development and testing of the smart sensors and virtual assistant.  
Photon Systems Instruments (PSI) has expertise in the development of NIR and MIR laser-based process analytical spectroscopy systems for industrial applications, e.g. in the food sector. PSI will develop the smart sensors and will ensure their integration into the monitoring and control systems of the processing lines.  
Polytec GmbH (POLY) has expertise in the development of NIR and MIR laser-based process analytical spectroscopy systems for industrial applications, e.g. in the food sector. POLY will develop the smart sensors and will ensure their integration into the monitoring and control systems of the processing lines.  
European Business Consulting GmbH (EBC) has expertise in the development of business models for the food industry. EBC will lead the development of the business models and will ensure their integration into the monitoring and control systems of the processing lines.
