1.1 12/09/2022 In order to address the following comment in the ESR: ‘The proposal indicates several 
barriers to outcomes and impacts and mitigation measures are determined properly. 
However, some regulatory issues related to validation of the proposed nanocoatings are not 
addressed to a full extent’, we have added the following statement in Table 15 on page 24: 
‘Regulatory issues related to validation of the proposed nanocoatings will be duly assessed 
during via the cocreation work that will be performed with industry in Task 4.1 Cocreation 
for an application-oriented development, as well as in WP5- Validation and Impact. We 
have a regulatory partner (UNE) in our consortium: their role is to address regulatory issues 
related to validation of the proposed nanocoatings.’  
 
In order to address the following comment in the ESR in relation to the ‘Do no significant 
harm principle’, whereby the experts stated ‘Given the particular character of (some) 
materials proposed to be used as components of the new anti-pathogen coatings, potential 
harm to humans and ecosystems cannot be fully excluded. There is no sufficient information 
provided at this stage to assess the full compliance of the proposal with “Do no significant 
harm” principle.’, we have added the following statement on page 17: ‘However, we will 
continually monitor this to ensure full compliance, and in particular this will be assessed in 
Task 2.3 Toxicity & ecotoxicity. We will also leverage the expertise of regulatory partner 
UNE.’ 
 
The Shortname of INTERPANE has been updated in Table 3.1h: ‘Purchase costs’ items 
(travel and subsistence, equipment and other goods, works and services) and ‘Other costs 
categories’ items (e.g. internally invoiced goods and services) on page 36.
