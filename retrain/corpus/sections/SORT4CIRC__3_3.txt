3.2.1 Consortium value chain overview  
All the 15 partners of SORT4CIRC will have a well-defined role in the project, allowing them to cover the entire value 
chain from technology providers to the end-users (Figure 12) 
Advisory Board (AB): SORT4CIRC will set up an AB, and interactions with the AB will occur at least twice a year. 
SORT4CIRC AB will include members from 4 different groups: Regulatory, overall value chain strategy, technical

aspects and recyclers. The entities who could be part of the Advisory Board of SORT4CIRC are shown in Figure 12. 
The AB members are chosen to ensure that results from SORT4CIRC will be relevant to the whole TVC. The AB will

3.2.2 Consortium description and complementarity  
To achieve its specific objectives, SORT4CIRC will gather a multidisciplinary consortium with the specific expertise 
of each participant to support the development of the project. The consortium comprises 14 beneficiaries and 1 
affiliated entity from 8 countries (Germany, Luxembourg, France, Belgium, the Netherlands, the United Kingdom, 
Slovakia, and Spain). 1 partner is RTOs, 3 partner is UNI, 10 partners are industrial companies (including 7 SMEs). 
 
Figure 12 - Value chain of the SORT4CIRC project 
CU: coordinator of the project - Constructor University’s three Schools convene scientists across various disciplines. 
CU research is characterised by multi- and interdisciplinarity, which is also the heart of its educational concept. The 
research group “EITIE” (Emerging Technologies in Industrial Engineering) has been active around blockchain 
technology and developing solutions for the sustainability of SC processes for almost 5 years. CU has a solid track 
record in Horizon Europe proj ect participation and management, including previous involvement in CRM - 
geothermal project, FAIRiCUBE project, PhotoCaM project, Progeny project, and EuropeanCity2 project. These 
projects have provided us with both the scientific and administrative experi ence required to coordinate complex 
international research collaborations. CU will coordinate the project (WP1) and lead the WP4, and all activities linked 
to the development of textile DPP. 
TXHO: Since beginning its operations over 20 years ago, Textile House has consistently built infrastructure and 
processes to support the reuse and recycling of post -consumer textiles in line with EU circular economy and waste 
policies, and it will continue to d evelop and scale innovative technologies for automated sorting, recognition, 
monitoring, measuring and classification. 
ARIS: AristEng S.à r.l. is an engineering consulting firm that provides tailor -made and fit-for-purpose engineering 
services to clients from diverse backgrounds. By linking research/experimental results/real production line data with 
process design and synthesis principles, they perform detailed techno-economic evaluation and environmental impact 
assessment of novel processes and new products at any project development phase, from embryonic lab -scale 
concepts to mature industrial -scale facilities. They will lead the tasks related to plantwide process model 
development, techno-economic evaluation and LCA. 
RECY: Recyc’Elit is a French start -up that has developed a patented chemical recycling process dedicated to 
complex polyester-based textiles. They will assess the quality of the sorting done in the project framework. In 2025, 
they will be equipped with several pilots / pre -industrial demonstrators. As recyclers, they will also provide the 
requirements/needs/specifications needed for WP4 (Textile DPP). 
RESO – Resortecs - provides DfD solutions that enable high-quality textile recycling on an industrial scale. RESO 
is the expert on 1) Eco-Design, DfD, and 2) Active-Disassembly in the textile industry, i.e. automatic disassembly of 
textile products to eliminate recycling disruptors (such as trims) from textile products, with no quality loss. RESO will 
use its expertise to develop DfD solutions over the DPP model (WP4).

IOM - The Leibniz Institute of Surface Engineering - is a non-profit, non-university research institute in the legal 
form of a registered association. The Materials Characterization and Analytics Group of IOM has dealt with 
developing methods for industrial process control based on NIR spectroscopy and NIR hyperspectral imaging. One 
more recent focus of this work was directed towards textiles, textile finishing, textile identification, etc. Those works 
were carried out with companies and industry research institutes. IOM will contribute to the development of a cost- 
effective and reliable inline multirange spectroscopic system for PSSR side of SORT4CIRC project (WP3). 
CEF - Circle Economy Foundation - is a non-profit impact organisation based in the Netherlands. CEF builds the 
evidence base and capacity of stakeholders to accelerate the uptake of circular solutions globally across industry and 
government. CEF will lead in the extraction of best practices across WPs, development of policy recommendations, 
as well as the organisation and facilitation of webinars. The affiliate 'Circularity Gap Solutions (CGS)' will support 
the project by providing experts who are able to provide their communications (i.e. social media content creation and 
management) and design support (i.e. visualisations, infographics, website design) to the project. The support from 
the experts in this affiliate entity will contribute to the services the project can provide to the beneficiaries. 
CEA - Commissariat à l’Energie Atomique et aux énergies alternatives - is a French research centre that develops 
technological solutions for industry. The CEA teams in Bordeaux and Saclay have strong expertise in developing 
intelligent imaging solutions explo iting images' spatial and spectral dimensions. The CEA has already developed 
expertise in analysing the composition of various textiles using hyperspectral imaging and will contribute to the 
development of PSSR system (WP3) 
SEM – SEMSO - is a machine manufacturer with strong experience in several sorting and recycling processes 
equipment. SEM has all the trades in-house, from the preliminary project to the start-up at the end customer. They 
have expertise in mechanical studies, electrical studies, automation studies, robotics studies, vision, manufacturing, 
assembly, wiring, finetune, delivery and after-sales service monitoring. SEM will use its expertise to design and build 
the metal frame for the automatised PSFS pilot and the integration of technologies into TXHO plants (WP2&5) 
PURFI - Purfi is the leader in fibre rejuvenation technology for spun fibres, focusing on the strategic categorisation 
and classification of textile waste and the subsequent processing of these materials using patented and trade secret- 
protected machinery and process techniques. Purfi is doing Soft Mechanical Recycling/Rejuvenation. The foundation 
of their technology is the rejuvenation of waste material back to virgin quality fibre, enabling it to be used in a variety 
of products and industries.  They will assess the quality of the  sorted garments and provide the 
requirements/needs/specifications for WP4 (Textile DPP). 
PSB- Paris School of Business - has a dedicated team of researchers with advanced decision -making and problem- 
solving abilities, supported by extensive expertise in quantitative research methods, including analytical, 
mathematical, and simulation modelling te chniques. PSB will propose and evaluate innovative BMs, emphasising 
the viability of circular business practices to provide actionable insights and robust solutions to drive sustainable 
business strategies. PSB will lead technical and economic feasibility, environmental and social assessments (WP6). 
TRA - The Textile Recycling Association - is the UK trade association for textile recycling. TRA will engage 
stakeholders through facilitating/organising relevant workshops/meetings and participating in sustainable textiles 
events to disseminate findings. TRA  will directly engage with policymakers at the national/international level and 
industry decision-makers, ensuring that lessons learnt from the research will be used to facilitate the delivery of new 
advanced textile sorting technologies & dramatically help to improve the economics of textile recycling (WP7). 
IRIS - Iris Technology Solutions - is the leading European company in manufacturing photonic and AI solutions to 
control industrial and production processes in real time. The expertise IRIS will bring to the project will be linked to 
recognising complex textiles through spectroscopy techniques. IRIS will develop a Multirange Spectroscopic 
Platform, which will be integrated into a semi-automatic sorting process and will lead WP3. 
REFA – reverse.fashion - is a company that provides technology for automated textile sorting. The AI-based software 
uses computer vision and enables the processing of DPP data to sort PCT into the most suitable reuse or recycling 
category. It increases the sorting efficiency and improves sorting quality according to the waste hierarchy. In this 
project, REFA develops its system further to retrieve product data by optical identification and similarity detection 
(WP2). Furthermore, it will provide access to its systems in WP5 for the demonstration of an automated sorting line. 
3.2.3 Resources distribution  
The SORT4CIRC project is composed of a total project cost of 5,097,767 € (and requested grant of 4,999,999 €). 
Partners are willing to contribute with in-kind resources as they foresee a huge commercial interest. The 
complementarity of the consortium is highlighted in Figure 12, and each partner will provide adequate resources for 
fulfilling their roles in the project, as highlighted in Table 3.1.f. The distribution of budget and grant is balanced 
among the partner types as follows: 39% for industries, 33% for SMEs, 23% for UNI and 5% for RTO. A major part 
of the budget (61%) is dedicated to direct personal costs, and the other parts are dedicated to indirect costs (20%), 
purchase costs (18%) and other cost categories (1%).

3.2.5 Industrial involvement  
SORT4CIRC has a complete value chain for designing and demonstrating an automated pre-sorting system. SORT4CIRC 
has secured a strong industrial commitment, the consortium including one main sorting actor in the textile field 
(TXHO) and 2 other industrial partners (SEM and PURFI) with a significant role in the project’s achievements to 
ensure industrial and commercial exploitation of the project results. CU is leading the overall project management 
(WP1). SEM will implement all technologies developed in WP3 and 4 into a demonstrator (WP5) in TXHO facilities. 
Recyclers (PURFI) will validate the innovative process, representing mechanical recycling (cotton, polycotton, etc.).
