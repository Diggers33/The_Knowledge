3.3 Consortium as a whole  
The AI-CUBE consortium has 5 partners representing 3 EU countries (Italy, Spain, Germany). All partners in 
the consortium have well -defined tasks and responsibilities and their technical and nontechnical expertise 
corresponds well to the requirements of each of the WP tasks. The main advantage of the consortium is that it 
combines different expertise and past experiences that are fundamental to reach the goals and targets as well 
as established and expected impact.   
The project will benefit from the work of each partner in related Innovation European and National Roadmaps 
(SPIRE, EFFRA, ALICE, etc) and this ensure the project will deliver a Roadmap which is compliant with the 
requirements derived also from the SPIRE Industry. Project partners have been directly involved in several 
research & development projects and among these the following ones re lated to AI and BD: SELIS 
(H2020MG-6.3-2015; N.690588), CONNECTED FACTORIES(H2020 -FOF-11-2015, n. 723777), 
AGROinLOG  
(H2020-RUR-08-2016; N.727961), FUTURING (H2020 -NMBP-CSA-2016), DISRUPT  (H2020 -FOF-
112016, n.723541), MOVAID (H2020 -FOF10-2015, n.680754) and indirectly through network members in 
many others. Among these, project INSPIRE (SPIRE-06-2016; N.: 723748) have 3 partners of the consortium 
are involved and was committed to find new business models to strengthen the collaboration between process 
 
 
industry and discrete manufacturing along supply chain collaboration. The link with INSPIRE is very strong 
and AI-CUBE can represent the follow-up of those activities.   
On top of that, partners and project team have participated in coordination actions, CSA, such as:  
MOBILITY4EU, SENSE, SETRIS, WINN, SoCool@EU, RECREATE, CRM_INNONET, BIO -TIC, 
BioLinX, NEW_InnoNet, EXPLORE, focussed on identifying policies, establishing collaboration 
frameworks, identifying best practices and development of roadmaps. The team has a strong expertise in supply 
chain management (ZLC), production processes (CNR), industrial symbiosis (PNO), data driven technologies 
(IML and IRIS), and so on. This expertise will be complemented with the support of the Expert Group that 
will provide their expertise in other relevant projects.   
The consortium has the ability to interface with the European Process Industry stakeholders. PNO is key 
partners to the European Technology Platform (ETP) SusChem and the European Council for the Chemical 
Industry (CEFIC). Both PNO and IRIS have strong ties with A.SPIRE, the SPIRE association. CNR-STIIMA 
is a major partners of the European Technological Platform on Future Manufacturing Technologies 
(MANUFUTURE) initiative and founding members of EFFRA. And ZLC promoted the European Technology 
Platform in Logistics, (ALICE) through the coordination of the EU-funded project WINN. IML are members 
of the Big Data Value Association (BDVA).  
The Consortium can directly exploit the national networks of each partner as a way to get directly in contact 
with the national research and innovation activities already running in the thematic areas of the project, as well 
as to significantly increase results diffusion.
3.4 Resources to be committed  
The duration of the AI -CUBE project will be 24  months. The total budget is 597.806,88 € of which the 
Commission’s contribution will be 597.806,88 €. This consists in 444.745,50 € for personnel costs, 33.500 € 
for other direct costs, and 119.561,38 € to cover the indirect costs. Furthermore, a strong network shall be built 
that will be enabled to sustain over the project duration and beyond. Dedicated dissemination actions toward 
the European process industries and ICT community shall engage the all stakeholders and raise awareness.   
All functions assigned in the project have gone through a thorough analysis of the prior experience and 
knowledge of each partner.   
The budgeted costs are predominantly personnel costs and minor quantities have been assigned to cover costs 
for travel and accommodation and costs in relation with dissemination, communication and exploitation 
activities. Additionally, budget to cover travel expenses for member s of the Expert Group has been allocated 
to the Coordinator’s budget. Within the Coordinator’s quota has also been allocated the budget for the AICUBE 
project website (€ 10,000), the creation of at least one video and infographic materials (€ 3,000) for 
dissemination purposes and the budget for Business Model Game workshop (€ 10,000).
