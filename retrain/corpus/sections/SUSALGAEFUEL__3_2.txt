3.2 Capacity of Participants and Consortium as a Whole #@CON-SOR-CS@# #@PRJ-MGT-PM@#          
SusAlgaeFuel will foster an international, multidisciplinary and multisectoral partnership between three universities, 
one research organisation, and five SMEs across five European countries (Ireland, Germany, Spain, Portugal, UK) 
(Figure 3.2a), with inclusion of relevant industry stakeholders providing direct pathways to commercialisation. The 
consortium also counts on the participation of Boeing and CityJet in its Advisory Board. 
University College Dublin (UCD) is Ireland’s largest university with 
a global research portfolio in sustainability and circular bioeconomy. 
UCD has dedicated research support units for administration, finance, 
legal, HR, ethics, and IP managemen t (NovaUCD), all of which will 
be availed for SusAlgaeFuel. Dr. Halim’s Group  is currently leading 
several projects to harness the power of microalgae in valorising waste 
products from agri -food sectors (dairy processing, brewery and AD 
operation). The  flow cytometer methods described in WP2 and  
autolytic incubation proposed in WP3 have been directly derived from 
the group’s findings. The Halim Group will transfer their technical 
know-hows in microalgae wastewater treatment, bacterial analysis and 
biorefinery processing to WPs1-3, WP6. The Group has access to core 
infrastructure in microscopy, flow cytometr y, chromatography. Dr. 
Syron’s Group has e xpertise in upscaling research for commercial 
deployment through the spin-out OxyMem. They will apply t his 
knowledge to process integration in WP5.  
ALGAFUEL SA ( A4F), Portugal, is a bioengineering company with over 20 years of experience in the Design, 
Build, Operation and Transfer (DBOT) of microalgae biotechnology. A4F  has an extensive  track record in 
microalgae, including the installation of the largest tubular photobioreactor units in Europe and successful 
coordination of FP7 & H2020 projects in microalgae biofuels (e.g. BIOFAT). A4F will apply their extensive technical 
know-hows in the production of microalgae at lab and pilot scales under autotrophic regimes to WP1 and WP6.  
Bia Energy Ltd (BIAE), Ireland, is a commercial AD operator with the capacity to process 99 ktonne p.a of organic 
waste for the production of 4.8 MW of renewable electricity. Dr. Nolan will lead BIAE’s participation and provide 
his expertise on AD systems optimisation. SusAlgaeFuel will benefit BIAE by providing a  potential commercial 
outlet for their CO2-rich effluent (from biogas upgrading) and digestate liquid fraction, both of which are significant 
pain points in their operation. There is currently no suitable outlet for the CO2 effluent in Ireland, while the significant 
volume of digestate generated  from biogas production (ca. 100 ktonnes p.a.) requires considerable land bank to 
adhere to EU Nitrates Directive (91/676/EEC) and thus accrue significant cost to the business. BIAE will contribute 
anaerobic digestate (200 kg p.a.) and provide the site for the construction of pilot-scale algal production facilities.   
IRIS Technology Solutions, S.L.  ( IRIS), Spain, is a manufacturer of real -time process monitoring solutions 
specialising in spectroscopy and artificial intelligence technology for engineering customised solutions for inline and 
online applications to transition food processing, chemical and biobased industries to Industry 4.0. IRIS has 
developed its own line of SWIR (shortwave infrared), hyperspectral imaging and Raman devices, in addition to data 
management and predictive software (Scadalytics). IRIS will use their multidisciplinary expertise in optica l 
engineering, chemometrics, electronics, and data science for the development of smart sensors in WP2 and WP6.   
Karlsruhe Institute for Technology (KIT) (Dr. Frey), Germany, has been involved in H2020 and national projects 
on cell-component recovery from microalgae (e.g. Sabana). The team will transfer their expertise in PEF treatment 
and microalgal biorefinery systems to WP3 in SusAlgaeFuel. The lab is fully equipped to accomplish the work , 
including bench-scale downstream processing auxiliaries (centrifuges up to 12 L capacity, disc separator STC 3-06-
107, HPH and bead-milling, freeze dryer), and diverse PEF-facilities (0.5 L/h up to 1,000 L/h).  
The University of Cambridge ( UCAM) (Dr. Marek) , UK, will apply their expertise in fuel synthesis and 
technologies on the development of algae HEFA-SPK route in WP4 and WP6. UCAM has the infrastructure needed 
to support WP4 activities and analysis for WP6, including two continuous high-pressure reactors and a batch reactor 
vessel operating up to 50 bar and at high temperatures , a separate rig for chemical looping production of H 2 and 
operation of up to 10 g of Fe-based catalyst, and material characterisation facilities (STEM, BET, XRD, TGA).  
Fraunhofer Gesellschaft is Europe’s largest research organisation  (Germany). The Umwelt-, Sicherheits- und 
Energietechnik (UMSICHT) division ( Dr. Apfelbacher)  will apply their expertise in  fuel transformation of 
lignocellulosic biomass by  TCR-HDO (TOSYNFUEL project) on microalgae biomass residue in WP 4 and WP6. 
UMSICHT houses specially built TCR reactors for hydrogenation at 400°C/200 bar at various scales (up to 500 kg/h).  
Sustainable Flight Solutions Ltd (SFS), Ireland, was founded in 2020 to promote the decarbonisation of the aviation 
sector through the development of SAF markets. SFS is perfectly positioned to lead the sustainability assessments in 
WP5 and disseminate project results, with an extensive network in the av iation industry, comprised of  SkyNRG, 
Avolon, Boeing, Orix Aviation, various Irish airlines and airport authorities.  
European Science Communication Institute  gGmbH (E SCI), Germany, is a not -for-profit organisation that 
empowers scientists to communicate with a non-expert audience using a message that not only tells, but also sells.  
They have accumulated significant experience in science communications, particularly in microalgae, through 
participation in various H2020/HE programme. They will lead the communication strategy of the project (WP7).
